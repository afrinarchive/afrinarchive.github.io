# Şiyê (شيخ الحديد)

Villages of Sheikh al-Hadid District / Şiyê

Sheikh al-Hadid (شيخ الحديد) - Şiyê

===

According to the book 'جبل الكرد' 'Mountain of the Kurds' (Afrin City) Geographical Study by Dr. Mohammed Abdo Ali (د. محمد عبدو علي):

Sheikh al-Hadid District: Sheikh al-Hadid district is located in the western part of the administrative map of Afrin City region, with its center being the town of Sheikh al-Hadid. It includes 18 villages and is divided administratively, two of which are abandoned. District boundaries: from the west Turkey, from the east Mabetli district, from the north Rajo district, and from the south Cindires district.

Şiyê, Sheikh al-Hadid (شيخ الحديد) /324m - 9828n/.

  • The name is attributed to the Şiyê valley located north of the town, and Şûya is a Kurdish word meaning "washing clothes and bathing," and this was done on the waters of that valley.
  • Its name appeared in ancient Islamic sources as "Şih al-Hadid" (شيح الحديد), while the designation "Sheikh al-Hadid" (شيخ الحديد) is a modern name for it.
  • Sheikh al-Hadid is a large town settled on the western slope of Ĝesê Xidir mountain. It was formed from several residential clusters: Şikaka in the south, and Hacê in the north, and the upper and lower villages Gundî jor û jêr.
  • In the center of the town is an abundant water spring, around which there were many building foundations, wells, old water channels and archaeological evidence of important urban installations dating back to pre-Islamic times, and they were buried under the new residential layers.
  • The town's inhabitants work primarily in olive cultivation. It has some olive presses and workshops for repairing agricultural machinery, commercial shops, a health center, and weekly markets held on Fridays. It is the birthplace of poet Hamid Badirkhan (حامد بدرخان) and he is buried there.

According to the book: 'عفرين .... نهرها ورواببها الخضراء' 'Afrin.... Its River and Green Hills' by writer Abdulrahman Mohammed (عبدالرحمن محمد) from Qatma village (قرية قطمه):

Sheikh al-Hadid District: A district in the Kurdish Mountain (جبل الكرد), belonging to Afrin City region, Aleppo governorate. It became a district center since 1975. Sheikh al-Hadid district is located in the western part of Afrin City region near the Turkish border directly. It is bordered to the north by Rajo district, to the south by Turkey border and Cindires district, to the east by Mabetli district, and to the west by Iskenderun province. It consists of Sheikh al-Hadid town and 13 villages and four farms.

Sheikh al-Hadid Town: A town in the Kurdish Mountain (جبل الكرد), belonging to Afrin City region, Aleppo governorate.

It is a large town located on the northwestern slope of the mentioned mountain, at the bottom of the western slope of an elevation called Sinara mountain (483m) crossed by several streams flowing west, and overlooks to the west plains covered with volcanic soil. It is 40km from Afrin City city westward. It is bordered to the north by a plain planted with olive trees and a mountain elevation and Arenda and Mistikanli and Sheikh Jaqallali villages, to the south by a fertile plain planted with olive trees and Sinara and Anqela villages, to the east by a high mountain chain planted with forest trees and Haj Hasanli and Ramadanli villages, to the west by a wide plain planted with olive trees and fruit trees and Qarmatlaq village. The number of its houses is about 800 houses and its age is about 800 years. The construction of the region dates back to ancient times, evidenced by the remains of basaltic columns, carved stones, old building foundations, pottery shards, and old water channels that cross the town.

Its dwellings are stone-clay with flat wooden roofs, which have been covered by modern cement dwellings in most directions. It has an electricity network and drinking water from an old water spring from the Roman era located in the northern part of the town and crosses the center of the town. Currently the town drinks from a water network that draws its water from an artesian well in its south and from rainwater collected in underground household cisterns. It has several elementary, preparatory and secondary schools and a telephone center. The municipality was established in 1979, a health center, a police station special for forest patrols, and a mosque in its center. A district was established in 1975, and a building was constructed for the district directorate headquarters. The inhabitants work in dry farming (olives, grains, legumes, vineyards) on an area of 1814 hectares and irrigated farming from artesian wells (vegetables, fruit trees) on an area of 566 hectares, in addition to their work in livestock raising. Some of the population also work in free professions. There are five olive presses in the town. It connects to the regional center by a paved road winding with neighboring villages. A weekly market is held every Friday in the center of the town (the bazaar).

The population of Sheikh al-Hadid town is 9558 people, and the total population of Sheikh al-Hadid district is about 29629 people. Among its most important families are the Sheikh Ismail Zada family (منان - كمال) - Kojer family (they came from Gomaita) - Doshar Alush - Hajo family - Al-Dankali family - Al-Ma'mali family - Al-Sheikho family (Sheikh Barim) - Al-Habash family (Habo).

Among the most important cultural figures we mention: the late poet Hamid Badirkhan (حامد بدرخان) - Engineer Mohammed Adoud (محمد أدود) who is the first agricultural engineer in Sheikh al-Hadid - Professor Mohammed Abdo (Ka'lo) (محمد عبدو (كعلو)) - Dr. Nouri Sheikh Ismail Zada (نوري شيخ اسماعيل زاده) the chemist who holds a doctorate in chemical sciences from Aleppo University and now teaches there - Dr. Hussein Adoud (حسين ادود) who holds a doctorate in informatics from France - Dr. Abdullah Mohammed (عبدالله محمد) who holds a doctorate in economics from France and teaches at Aleppo University.

Village mayor: Said Kojer (سعيد كوجر) - Behjat Bakr Bakr (بهجت بكر بكر).
