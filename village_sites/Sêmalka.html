I. Summary from TirejAfrin Site of Sêmalka (English)

According to the book 'جبل الكرد' 'Mountain of the Kurds' (عفرين) Geographic Study by Dr. Mohammed Abdo Ali:

Sêmalka (سمالك), Semalka (سمالك), Al-Samal (السمال) /949n - 550ha - 11km - 520m/:

  • Semalka (سيمالك): Meaning "three houses", and the village in the beginning of its formation consisted of three houses. The Arabic name "Al-Sahal" (السحال) does not mean anything.
  • A medium village located on the southern slope of Hawar mountain (جبل هاوار).

According to the book: Afrin... Its River and Green Hills by writer Abdul Rahman Mohammed (عبدالرحمن محمد) from Qatmah village (قرية قطمه):

Semalka (سمالك): A village in Mountain of the Kurds (جبل الكرد), belonging to Mabetli District (ناحية المعبطلي), Afrin City region (منطقة عفرين), Aleppo Governorate (محافظة حلب).

It is a small village located in the central part of the mentioned mountain on a high rocky limestone slope crossed by several small streams descending northward, westward, eastward, and southward toward the agricultural lands with fertile clay soil. It is 16km away from the district center toward the northeast. It is bounded to the north by a harsh and rough slope and Sili valley (وادي سيلي) and high mountain elevations of Hawari mountain (جيل هواري) and Tag Oba Si village (قرية طاغ أوبه سي) at the mountain peak, and to the south by a slope planted with olive trees and Kamruk village (قرية كمروك), and to the west by a slope and Sili valley (وادي سيلي) and a wide fertile plain planted with olive trees and Abruz village (قرية أبرز) and Sheikh Kilo village (شيخ كيلو), and to the east by a slope and Sili valley (وادي سيلي) and Shorba Oghli village (قرية شوربه أوغلي). The number of its houses is about 75 houses and its population is about 450 people. Its old dwellings are stone-clay with flat wooden roofs and the modern ones are cement spreading southward and westward to the main road Rajo junction (مفرق راجو) and to the Bulbul road (طريق بلبل). It has an electricity network and water network connected to the well of Shorba Oghli village (قرية شوربه أوغلي) located south of Shorba village (قرية شوربه). It has an elementary school and mosque. It is connected to the district center by a paved road to the center of the village. There is a modern olive press in it. Its inhabitants work in rain-fed agriculture (grains, olives, legumes) on an area of 300 hectares and irrigated from artesian wells with summer vegetables and pomegranate and walnut trees, in addition to raising sheep and goats. Among its most important families are the families of Jamu (جامو), Hamo (حمو), and Abdul Aziz (عبد العزيز) and others (the first to inhabit the village). It consisted of only three houses, so it was called Semlek village (قرية سيملك) in relation to these three brothers.

Among the families in the village: Al Abdul Aziz (آل عبدالعزيز) - Al Hamajah (آل حماجه) - Al Sheikh Kila (آل شيخ كيله) - Al Hanu (آل حنو) - Al Konu (آل كونو)

Among the social figures in the village is Professor Youssef Abdul Aziz (الأستاذ يوسف عبدالعزيز)

Village mayor: Khalil Sheikh Kilo (خليل شيخ كيلو)
