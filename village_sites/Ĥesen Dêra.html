I. Summary from TirejAfrin Site

# Ḧesen Dêra Village (حسن ديرلي)

According to the book 'جبل الكرد' / 'Mountain of the Kurds' / Afrin City / Geographical Study:

Ḧesendêra (ḧesendêra), Hasan Deirli (حسن ديرلي), Deir Hasan (دير حسن) /1947N - 480M150H - 17KM/:

  • Hasan Daira (حسن ديرا): Hasan (حسن): the name of the first person who settled in the location, so the village was named after him and the archaeological attribute of the place (Deir (دير)).
  • A medium village located at the bottom of the eastern slope of Hawar (هاوار) mountain. It is separated from the mountain by Ashuna (عشونة) valley. There are caves and engravings carved in rock and scattered ruins near it on the eastern slope of Hawar (هاوار) mountain.

According to the book 'عفرين .... نهرها ورواديها الخضراء' 'Afrin.... Its River and Green Valleys':

Hasan Deirli (حسن ديرلي): A village in Mountain of the Kurds (جبل الكرد) belonging to Bilbile (بلبل) district, Afrin City (عفرين) region, Aleppo (حلب) governorate.

It is a large and ancient village located at the bottom of the notched eastern slope of the limestone "Hawara" (هوارة) mountain, where the slope is gentle and the soil is clay suitable for agriculture and grazing, 17 km southeast of Bilbile (بلبل) town. It is bordered to the north by "Hawari" (هواري) mountain and Quri Kul (قوري كول) village, to the south by a stream valley and high mountain chain and Shorba Oghli (شوربة أوغلي) village, to the west by a stream valley and Hawari (هواري) mountain and Sheikhlir Oba Si (شيخلر أوبة سي) village, and to the east by a stream valley and Naza Oshaghi (نازا أوشاغي) village. The number of houses is about 125 and its age is about 400 years. Its old houses are stone and clay with flat wooden roofs and the modern ones are cement expanding eastward. It has an electricity network and a primary school and a mosque in the center of the village. Most of its residents work in olive, grain and legume cultivation rain-fed (150 hectares) in addition to raising sheep and goats. Its people drink from cisterns that collect rainwater. The road from there to Bilbile (بلبل) town is paved. Among the families in the village are (Bakru (باكرو) (Bakra) – Siya (سَيا) (family of the late artist Ali Tajo (علي تجو)) – Khalma (خَالمَا) – Kajklara (كجكلارا) – Haska (حسكا)) and among the holders of higher degrees in the village are Ms. Zinat Khalil daughter of Safar (الآنسة زينات خليل بنت سفر) (Master's in French Language from Aleppo University (جامعة حلب) and Zalukh Khalil daughter of Safar (زلوخ خليل بنت سفر) Master's in French Language) and among the social figures (Ismat Sheikho (عصمت شيخو) - Abdul Karim Rashid / Abu Rizan (عبد الكريم رشيد / أبو ريزان) - Mohammed Hasu / Abu Taj (محمد حسو / أبو تاج) - Abdul Karim Hasu / Abu Shafan (عبد الكريم حسو / أبو شفان)) – the late artist Mohammed Ali Tajo (محمد علي تجو) – the poet Barkhdan Abdul Karim Hasu (برخدان عبد الكريم حسو) – and the graduate in Mathematics (Informatics) and educational qualification diploma Professor Mohammed Hasu son of Aref (الاستاذ محمد حسو بن عارف) / owner of Al-Yasmin Computer Office (مكتب الياسمين للكمبيوتر), and among the early fighters the late Abdul Hamid Mustafa Atta (عبد الحميد مصطفى عطة) – and among the professionals Mr. Khalil Rasho Abu Bahri (السيد خليل رشو أبو بحري) who is considered among the first weapon repairers in the region.

II. Summary from Ax û Walat Transcript

The village of Hesen Dêra is noted for its historical significance (01:00). It is described as one of the first villages in the Efrîn region, an old, historical village inhabited for three to four hundred years (03:16-03:22), and was originally built in an area with streams (03:30-03:39). The founders were from the Hesena tribe (03:50-04:02), a large, ancient tribe with a history of battles, once led by Seyîd Xan (04:05-04:28). The village's name, Hesen Dêra, derives from the Hesena tribe and "dêr" (church), as there was an existing church (Kenîse) and Roman ruins ("eserê rûm") when the tribe settled there (04:40-04:55, 05:04-05:09). This church was a Roman site (05:15). A significant well in the village was dug in 1970 following an agreement between village elders and the local government (06:14-06:35). In the past, education was provided by "xoce" (teachers) who taught children, including "eski Türkçe" (Old Turkish), letters, and the Quran, as formal schools did not exist (07:11-07:15, 07:30-07:39, 07:50-08:03). The village is rich in archaeological sites, with visible remains from the Hurrian and Roman periods, and is located 20 km west of Kela Horî (Sîrûs) (13:23-13:27). On the nearby Çiyayê Hawarê, a 300-400 year old relief, originally called Sûrêta Xizikî, was later identified as Şimşûn (Samson) by Christian scholars from Damascus and Lebanon around 300-400 years ago; this identification appeared on their maps (30:38-31:11). Şimşûn was considered Jewish or from Bani Israel (31:31). Near this relief, there are a spring and signs of an old castle, and discovered caves and wells suggest very old settlements (32:01-32:07, 32:12-32:19). A large cave, Şikefta Bûko (Bride's Cave), was an ancient place for champions and was used in ancient times for keeping sheep during weddings; Yazidis also used these caves (33:01-33:05, 33:22-33:36, 33:42-33:44). During the Hurrian period, Yazidi believers held religious ceremonies in Şikefta Bûkê (37:18-37:20). The cave was also reportedly a site of conflict between aghas and wealthy individuals (37:27-37:29). The area also features springs like Ava Xoceyê Mehmûd, believed to have healing properties (35:28-35:42), and Kenîyo Bekirê Xelî (36:24).
