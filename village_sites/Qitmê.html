I. Summary from TirejAfrin Site of Qitmê (English)

According to the book 'جبل الكرد' 'Mountain of the Kurds' / Afrin City / Geographic Study:

Qitmê, Qatma (قطمه) /5749n - 988h - 4km - 650m/:

  • Qatma (قطمة): It is believed that the name is of Turkish origin meaning "don't mix", and there is a folk tale that speaks of the name's connection to the birth of "Tamerlane" (تيمورلنك), the famous Mongol leader.
  • A large, prosperous village located on the southern slope of a limestone plateau. Next to it is the train station known by its name. There is a large archaeological tell 800m south of the village, containing foundations of buildings made of massive carved masonry, with pottery shards scattered on its surface. The northern and eastern elevations overlooking it have been forested. It has a forest department and a railway station. The village was an important headquarters for Ottoman and later French forces. From it comes former People's Assembly (مجلس الشعب) member Ahmed Mukhtar (أحمد مختار), and Ba'ath Party (حزب البعث) branch leadership member in Aleppo (حلب) Dr. Rashdi Mukhtar (رشدي مختار).

According to the book 'عفرين .... نهرها وروابيها الخضراء' 'Afrin.... Its River and Green Hills':

Qatma (قطمه): A village in Jabal al-Akrad (جبل الكرد), belonging to Sheran District (شران), Afrin (عفرين) region, Aleppo Governorate (حلب).

It is a large village located in the eastern part of Jabal al-Akrad (جبل الكرد) and the northern part of Mount Simaan (جبل سمعان) on the southern slope of a limestone plateau. It is 4km from the town of Sheran (شران) toward the south and 1km from the Aleppo-Rajo (حلب راجو) railway line toward the north. Several streams flow from it southward, joining a large stream heading west toward the Afrin River (عفرين) valley. It is connected by a fertile plain. The region's construction is very ancient, evidenced by the presence of a large archaeological tell south of the village 800m away on building foundations made of massive carved masonry. Pottery shards are also scattered on its surface.

It is bordered to the north by mountainous elevations planted with forest trees and the villages of Sinkarli (سنكرلي) and Khirbet Sheran (خربة شران), to the south by a slope and plain and the railway line and archaeological tell and the Afrin-Aleppo (عفرين حلب) road and modern villas on the public road and the villages of Ma'rasth al-Khatib (معرسة الخطيب) and Al-Malikiya (المالكية), to the west by a valley and Hamouda (حمودة) elevations and Sadiq (صادق) and Manan (منان) farms and the village of Kafr Jana (كفر جنة), and to the east by Sa'd Valley (وادي سعد) and a wide fertile plain agriculturally planted with olive and grape trees and the cities of A'zaz (أعزاز), Sijraz (سيجراز), and Baflion (بافليون). It has about 300 houses and is approximately 500 years old according to statements by one of the elderly residents/100/years. Its old houses are stone and clay with flat wooden roofs surrounded by modern cement construction. It has an electricity network and drinking water from the state-owned Al-Malikiya (المالكية) well, as well as a drinking water well from the Roman era at the bottom of the village near the railway. It has elementary and preparatory schools, a modern mosque in the village center, a post center, and a train station east of the village, considered one of the most beautiful northern stations. There is a youth production camp in the northern elevations of the village and a water spring next to the Roman well that flows into Kafr Jana Valley (وادي كفر جنة) and Afrin City. There has been a municipality building since 1983 in addition to 8 modern technical mills.

The village residents work in rain-fed agriculture (grains - olives - grapes - vegetables) on an area of 988 hectares. The northern, eastern, and western elevations overlooking it have been forested with forest trees. It has a forest department. It connects with the district center by paved road and two main paved streets in the village. Currently, buildings have spread around the public road and several modern luxurious villas. It has the temporary headquarters of Al-Ma'moun University for Science and Technology (جامعة المأمون للعلوم والتكنولوجيا) /formerly, and this site was called Qatma Al-Jadida Suburb (ضاحية قاطمة الجديدة), where there is the famous Mari Restaurant (مطعم ماري) and several cafes and a technical services center belonging to the Technical Services Directorate (مديرية الخدمات الفنية) in Aleppo. It is considered one of the beautiful villages in the Afrin (عفرين) region. Among its most important families: the Ma'junoo (معجونو) family - the first to inhabit the village, and the Ahmed Hassan (أحمد حسن) family. Its population according to the civil registry dated 31/12/2005 is (5,750 people). In 1922, Qatma (قاطمة) was an important center for the Jabal al-Akrad (جبل الكرد) region during the French mandate, then moved to Midanki (ميدانكي).

Qatma Al-Jadida Suburb (ضاحية قطمة الجديدة): A new suburb in Jabal al-Akrad (جبل الكرد), belonging to Qatma Village (قاطمة), Sheran District (شران), Afrin (عفرين) region, Aleppo Governorate (حلب). It is a small and modern suburb. The area between the junction of Qatma Train Station (محطة قطار قطمة) and the Kafr Jana (كفر جنة) junction, 2km long, was transformed into a residential area of excellent class, extending alongside the Afrin-Aleppo (عفرين – حلب) public road to dozens of luxurious and beautiful villas and several restaurants and cafes. The most important are Mari Restaurant (مطعم ماري) and Al-Jamil Palace (قصر الجميل), which became the center of Al-Ma'moun University for Science and Technology (جامعة المأمون للعلوم والتكنولوجيا) and the College of Administrative Sciences (كلية العلوم الإدارية) established in 2006, in addition to several service workshop shops that serve passersby, vacationers, and residents living there.

Among the families in the village: Al Kur Ali (كور علي) - Al Salu (سلو) - Al Dada (دادا) - Al Khalil (خليل) - Al Hasu (حسو) - Al Mukhtar (مختار) - Al Hashash (حشاش) - Al Ali Sanma (علي سانمه) - Al Abda Uska (عبده اوسكه) (Haji Othman (حاجي عثمان) - website director Tereij Afrin (تيريج عفرين) from this family) - Al Usku (اوسكو) - Al Jalal (جلال) - Al Sheikh (شيخ)

III. Summary from other Channels' Transcripts of Qitmê

C. Summary from Afrin 366 Transcript of Qitmê

The village of Qitme (Gundê Qitmê), located on the road to Aleppo opposite Kafr Janneh, is a historic settlement of 350 to 375 households, estimated to be between 650 and 700 years old. The origin of its name is traced back to a Turkish word, with two different explanations provided by the speakers. According to the main narrator, 'Qitme' derives from the Turkish phrase 'qatma qatma,' meaning 'don't interfere' or 'don't bother.' However, an elderly resident offered a more specific historical account, explaining that the name comes from the Turkish word for 'do not sever/cut' ('qut meke'). He stated this was because the village was the site of a critical railway junction for the 'shemendefer' train, where one line branched off to Meydan Ekbis and another continued to Aleppo. The village itself shows a history of growth, as residents distinguish between an 'old Qitme' (Qitmê kevin) and a 'new Qitme' (Qitmê nû), which includes an upper neighborhood (xorata jorî).
