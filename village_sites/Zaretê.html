I. Summary from TirejAfrin Site

According to the book 'جبل الكرد' 'Mountain of the Kurds' geographical study by Dr. Mohammed Abdo Ali:

Zaretê, جتل زيارة, الزيارة /1906n - 370h - 35km - 481m/:

  • چتل زيارة: It is the name before Arabization, and means in Turkish "the double shrine", and that is attributed to two shrines existing in it, one of them known by the name of Sheikh Youssef al-Koufi, and he is present inside the village cemetery. The Kurds shortened the name and pronounced it "زارێتي".
  • A large village located on the eastern slope of Lilon Mountain with a large percentage of holders of higher academic degrees and state employees. Among them is the writer and literary figure Imad Kusa.

And it came in the book: Afrin.... its river and green hills by writer Abdulrahman Mohammed from Qatma village

جتال زيارة: A village in Lilon Mountain, belonging to the center villages district and Afrin region, Aleppo governorate.

It is a large village located in the Lilon Mountain chain and the dusty region. It is bordered to the north by a slope and valley and agricultural plain and Iben village and Baslahaya and to the south by a mountain chain and rough terrain and Kharabat al-Qanitera and Miyasa and Nabl town and to the east by a slope and valley and Shahba farm and Aleppo Afrin road and Kafin village and to the west by a mountain chain and forestry and Aqiba village. It belongs to the center villages district Afrin Aleppo. The number of its houses reaches 150 houses and its age is 400 years. Its old houses are of stone and wooden and clay roofs and the modern ones are of stone and cement. It has electricity network and drinking water and elementary school and mosque and belongs to Aqiba municipality and an asphalt road connects to Aleppo road and passes through its center to the rest of the neighboring villages until Basuta village. Its inhabitants work in agriculture and especially grain cultivation - and olives and vineyards - and livestock and goats and cattle raising because of the mountainous nature of the surrounding area called Lilon Mountain. Currently modern houses have spread on both sides of the road until the Aleppo - Afrin - A'zaz and al-Ra'i junction and the scattered chalets on the road called Shahba farm located east of the village with a gas station. Among the families existing in the village (Al Kusa - Al Ibish - Al Ahmad/Isu - Al Hassan/Hamuto - Al Walio - Al Youssef - Al Hamido - Al Bakri - Al Awso) and among the cultural and scientific personalities (writer Imad Kusa - Dr. Faisal Ahmed/doctorate in economics from Aleppo University) and many holders of university degrees. The village mayor is Zakaria Juma'a al-Hassan and he is one of the prominent social figures in the village and has a position among the village people.

II. Summary from Ax û Walat Transcript

Summary: An elder states that for nearly four years, the people of Afrin have been displaced to Shahba (03:12). Another man discusses the agricultural history of the Sherawa region, noting that about forty years ago, their village did not have olive trees and they learned to cultivate them from the people of Afrin proper (07:02 - 07:22). Before that, their farming was similar to that on Mount Lêlûn, consisting of wheat and lentils (07:29). He also mentions that 100 years ago, wild olives grew on Mount Lêlûn (08:31). A traditional bonesetter ("cebarcî") explains that in the past, villages relied on local healers instead of doctors (13:12) and that he learned his trade from a now-deceased man from the village of Aqîbê (13:40). The speakers discuss the Aleppo boil ("hebê Helebê"), a historically known ailment from the Aleppo region (18:18), and note that their knowledge of traditional remedies was passed down from their ancestors (20:13, 21:21). A man from the village of Ba'dîna recalls making grape molasses using traditional fire pits, a practice he has done for 35 years but can no longer do since being displaced (29:35, 30:14). The speakers state they have been displaced from Afrin for six years, leaving behind their ancestral olive and grape groves (31:19, 32:18). A musician reminisces about being in the music group "Koma Aşiqî" and names several influential artists from that era, including Şahîban and Ednan Dilbirîn (37:36), noting that older artists were driven by a love for the art rather than money (38:41). The history of a folkloric song is traced back to artists like Elî Kopê and Bavê Salah, highlighting the oral and artistic traditions of the region (40:38 - 40:56).

Summary from secondary Ax û Welat Video:

The village history dates back over 1300 years to the early Islamic period when Şêx Yûsif El-Kûfî and Şêx Mihemed El-Kûfî from Kufa were martyred here; shrines were built over their graves (33:43-34:45). The original village name was Zaretê Şêx Yûsif (05:28). The current lineage traces back about 300 years to Kolwelî from the Millî tribe, who arrived from Sêrêkaniyê (journey 1670-1710) (06:24-06:37, 07:09-07:17). Historically, the village was a key stop with khans (one established ~300 years ago) on the trade route between the Efrin/Antep area and Aleppo, before modern borders were drawn (24:16-26:45, 28:24). During the Ottoman era, the name was changed to Çetel Ziyaret due to the two shrines (05:34-05:45). The khans ceased operation around 150-200 years ago with the advent of borders and modern transport (28:56-29:25). An Arab family settled here over 80 years ago (23:19-23:25). In recent history, ISIS destroyed the shrines in 2013 (10:56-11:05, 36:05-36:12). The village suffered from conflict, including fatal regime airstrikes (2013, 2016) and Russian bombing, before YPG/YPJ liberation on Feb 4, 2016 (31:43-31:52, 40:56-41:41).
