I. Summary from TirejAfrin Site of Tilifê (English)

According to the book 'جبل الكرد' 'Mountain of the Kurds', a geographical study by Dr. Mohammed Abdo Ali:

Tilfê, تللف /811n - 290e - 10km - 345m/:

  • Tilf: In Kurdish means the remnants of olives and grapes after pressing them /Kurdistan Dictionary/, and the village is located amidst vast fields of olive trees.
  • A small village located on the western slope of the Qaraj Tilf elevation. It is inhabited by the Zeinel family, relatives of Hassan Effendi from the 18th century. It has an important workshop for manufacturing some agricultural machinery requirements.

According to the book: Afrin.... its river and green hills by writer Abdul Rahman Mohammed from Qutmeh village:

تللف: A village in Jebel al-Kurd, belonging to the central villages district and Afrin region, Aleppo governorate.

It is a medium-sized village located on the western slope of the Karaj Tilf elevation (380m), at the beginning of a watercourse flowing toward the northwest on clay soil. It is located 10km to the southwest of Afrin city. It is bordered to the north by a slope and fertile agricultural plain and the Afrin-Jandaris road and Kafr Dal Li Tahtani village, to the south by a slope and plain planted with olive trees and Kafr Zait village, to the west by a slope and agricultural plain planted with olive trees and Sheikh Abdul Rahman and al-Azeiriya villages, and to the east by a mountainous elevation planted with olive trees and Kokbeh and Bozikeh villages. The number of its houses is approximately 45 houses and its age is about 400 years. Its old dwellings are stone and clay with wooden roofs, while the modern ones are stone made of reinforced cement. Modern construction has spread from all directions. It has an electricity network and an elementary school and a paved road passing through its center to the nearby Kafr Zait village. The residents drink from artesian wells in the house courtyards or water collection pools. Its inhabitants work in olive cultivation, vineyards, grains, and vegetables, and they raise sheep. Its lands are very fertile agricultural soil surrounded by olive trees from all sides. Among its most important families are the Zeinel and Hassan al-Effendi families.

Village headman: Imad al-Din Zeinel

II. Summary from Ax û Walat Transcript of Tilifê

The village of Tilfê, located in the Cindirês district, was founded over 100 years ago by a single family lineage that migrated from the village of Gazi in Duderiyê. The community's primary ancestor is identified as Elî Beg, son of Qeyso Axa and grandson of Hesen Efendî, and nearly all inhabitants descend from him, with only one or two families having joined later from other villages such as Çolaqa. The name Tilfê is believed to derive either from the Kurmanji phrase "dordorê gir" (around the hill) or the older name "Til-Lev." The cultural life of the village is deeply connected to the tradition of Dengbêj, with local artists like Tûzîal performing songs that honor cultural figures and martyrs, such as the renowned singer Şehîd Serhad. This region also produced social pioneers like Mamoste Necah Silêman from the nearby village of Mîrkan, who became the first woman in the Afrin Canton to pursue an education in 1944 and subsequently became its first female teacher in 1957, a significant achievement given the social barriers of the time. Economically, the village has evolved from purely traditional agriculture to include modern industries; a local workshop named "Zuher Afrin," established around 15 years ago, now manufactures and exports olive oil presses to other Syrian provinces, Jordan, and Lebanon, marking a shift from importing such machinery from Turkey and Italy.

II. Summary from Ax û Walat Transcript of Tilifê 2

The village of Tilfê, located in the Cindirêsê district of Afrin Canton, possesses a rich oral history detailing its origins, cultural life, and modern development. Founded over a century ago by Hesen Efendî, an ancestor who migrated from the village of Gozê, Tilfê was established on former state land purchased from local figures Îsa Axa and Elî Beg. The entire village community descends from a single progenitor, Elî Beg, and has grown from an initial five households to over sixty. The village's name is derived from a local landmark, a hill known in Kurdish as "Girê liv" (moving hill) or "Serê Kût," which also serves as the site for communal Newroz celebrations where villagers gather to light a festival fire. This cultural life is further enriched by a strong artistic tradition, exemplified by the family of singer Tûzê Hel, which includes several other musicians who perform and adapt historical songs, such as those originally by Cemîl Horo and later popularized by Martyr Serhad. A defining feature of Tilfê's modern identity is its profound emphasis on education, a legacy embodied by Necah Silêman, who broke social barriers in the 1940s as the first girl to attend her local school and later became the first female teacher in the entire Kurd Mountains region in 1957. This commitment to learning has resulted in the small village producing a remarkable number of professionals, including over twenty-one doctors, twelve engineers, and numerous pharmacists and lawyers. The village's economy has also evolved, transitioning from traditional manual olive presses ("celoqî") to modern industrial machinery that once exported products under the brand "Zuher Afrin" to Syria, Jordan, and Lebanon, alongside contemporary agribusiness like poultry farming.
