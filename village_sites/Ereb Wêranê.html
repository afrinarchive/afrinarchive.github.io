I. Summary from TirejAfrin Site of Ereb Wêranê (English)

According to the book جبل الكرد|Mountain of the Kurds / Afrin City / Geographic Study:

'Erebwêran (عرب ويران) (al-Aruba العروبة) /426N - 569E - 565m/:

  • Arab Weiran (عرب ويران): Meaning "ruins of the Arabs", because Weiran (ويران): means "ruin or tuberculosis" in the Kurdish language. There is no connection for the Arabs to this small village far from politics.
  • A small village 2km away from the Turkish border. It is surrounded by artificial pine forests from the south, and it is one of the villages of Haj Omar aghas.

According to the book عفرين.... نهرها ورواييها الخضراء|Afrin.... Her River and Green Hills:

Arab Weiran (عرب ويران): A village in Jabal al-Kurd (جبل الكرد) belonging to Sheran District (ناحية شران), Afrin Region (منطقة عفرين), Aleppo Governorate (محافظة حلب), (411 N).

It is a very small village located in the northern part of the mentioned mountain mass and on the northeastern slope of a limestone plateau with waves cut by streams descending north and west to the Afrin River (نهر عفرين) valley, with clayey soil. It is 3km west of the Turkish border and 8km from the town of Sheran (شران) in the northeast direction. It is bounded to the north by slope and mountain chains and valleys and the ancient Roman archaeological bridge and the village of Deir Sawan (دير صوان). To the south by mountain chains and valleys planted with olive trees and the village of Qastal Jindo (قسطل جندو). To the east by a deep valley and mountain chain planted with forest trees and olives and a beautiful farm and the village of Dikmeh Tash (ديكمه طاش). To the west by mountain chain planted with forest trees and the valley of Omranli Village (قرية عمرانلي). The number of its houses is about 20 houses and its age is about 200 years. In the past it had one family, the family of Mohammed Daran (محمد دران). It has an electricity network and a paved road passes through the village to Deir Sawan (دير صوان) and Nabi Houri (النبي هوري). The residents drink from artesian wells and cisterns. Its houses are modern, made of cement and stone. Its residents work in olive and grape cultivation and livestock raising.
