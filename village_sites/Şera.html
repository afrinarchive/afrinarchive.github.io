I. Summary from TirejAfrin Site

According to the book 'جبل الكرد' 'Mountain of the Kurds' / Afrin City / Geographic Study:

Sheran District (شران) includes 44 administrative divisions for residential communities, four of which are names for abandoned locations. The district center is the town of Sheran (شران), bordered by: from the east the A'zaz (أعزاز) region, from the south the Central District (المركز), from the west the districts of Maabatli (معبطلي) and Bulbul (بلبل), and from the north the Turkish borders.

Şera, Sheran (شران) /2381n - 480h/:

  • Şer means fighting in Kurdish. And Şarri in the ancient Hurrian language means king/leader of the people, p.170/.
  • The town of Sheran (شران) is located on the western slope of a limestone plateau. It is 13km from Afrin City (عفرين) in the northeastern direction. The region's construction is ancient, evidenced by the presence of massive carved limestone masonry, graves, and wells carved in rock dating back to the Roman era. It neighbors the village of Khirbet Sheran (خربة شران) to the south, which is the old village, separated by Wadi Entûz ('Entûz) coming from the east.

The residents work in agriculture as a primary source of livelihood, surrounded by olive groves and fruit orchards. It has commercial shops and small workshops for maintenance, blacksmithing, carpentry, building materials, and olive presses.

Sheran (شران) is a beautiful town, its houses sloping gently toward the west and south, and some modern buildings with elegant villas have begun to appear.

According to the book 'عفرين .... نهرها وروابيها الخضراء' 'Afrin.... Its River and Green Hills':

Sheran District (ناحية شران): It is the district center with an area of /331.35/ km2 and a population according to the civil registry dated 31/12/2004 of (50,897 people). It is bordered from the east by the A'zaz (أعزاز) region, from the south by villages of the Central District (المركز) /Afrin City/, from the west by the districts of Maabatli (معبطلي) and Bulbul (بلبل), and from the north by the Turkish borders and villages of Bulbul District (بلبل). It includes /37/ villages and /7/ farms. The district was established according to administrative divisions in 1975. Tell al-Mikhfar (تل المخفر) was an important center for French forces during the French mandate over Syria. We will discuss in detail each village separately belonging to the district.

Town of Sheran (بلدة شران): A town in Jabal al-Akrad (جبل الكرد), it is the district center, belonging to the Afrin (عفرين) region, Aleppo Governorate (حلب).

The town of Sheran (شران) is located in the northeastern part of Jabal al-Akrad (جبل الكرد), on the western slope of a limestone plateau at elevation /500/m. It is an old village that slopes gently toward the south and is fronted by a seasonal valley heading toward the Afrin River (عفرين) valley. It is connected by a fertile plain and is about /13/ km from Afrin City (عفرين) in the northeastern direction. The region's construction is ancient, evidenced by the presence of massive carved limestone masonry, graves, and wells carved in rock dating back to the Roman era.

It is bordered to the north by mountainous elevations planted with olive trees and the village of Jamanli (جمانلي), to the south by a slope and seasonal valley and the village of Khirbet Sheran (خربة شران) and Matinli (متينلي) at 1km distance, to the east by a slope and valley and fertile plain planted with olive, walnut, and pomegranate trees and the village of Sinkarli (سنكرلي) at 1km distance, and to the west by mountainous elevations planted with olive and forest trees and the village of Kupalak (كوبه لك). It has about /110/ houses and is approximately /500/ years old. Its old houses are stone and clay with wooden roofs, and the modern ones are stone and cement. Some modern buildings with elegant villas have begun to appear toward the western and northern sides and along both sides of the main street in the town center, starting from the south at the first town entrance near Khirbet Sheran (خربة شران) at the Victory Arch (قوس النصر), with several luxurious villas on both sides of the road.

The town's population at the end of 2004 reached about /2500/ people, and the total population in the district reached /50,897/ people. It has an electricity network, elementary and preparatory schools, a secondary school, an old mosque, a post and telephone center, a health center, a conscription branch, a party branch, an old police station, and a building for the district directorate and municipality. There is also one technical mill and several regular mills. It has commercial shops and small workshops for maintenance, blacksmithing, and building materials sales.

The town's residents work in rain-fed agriculture (olives and grains) and irrigation from artesian wells (walnuts, apples, cherries, and summer vegetables) alongside raising sheep and goats. The town drinks water from a water network connected to the artesian well south of the town at the bottom of the valley. There are also several paved streets. The town connects with the regional center by paved road and passes through its center to several neighboring villages. Sheran (شران) is a beautiful town with houses sloping gently toward the west. Among its most important families: the family of (Manan Agha Jelosi (مناان آغا جلوسي)) - they were the first to inhabit the village until today. Its population according to the civil registry dated 31/12/2004 is (2,332 people), and the first to assume management of Sheran District (شران) directorate was Abdul Qader Khatib (عبد القادر خطيب).

Among the families present in the village: Al Jelosi (جلوسي) - Al Ainalo (اينالو) - Al Aibsh (ايبش) - Al Qarhaman (قره مان) - Al Musalli (مصللي) - Al Ma'dan (معدان) (Sha'bu)

Village mayor: Jamal Jabo (جمال جابو)
