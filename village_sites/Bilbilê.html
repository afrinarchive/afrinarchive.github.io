I. Summary from TirejAfrin Site of Bilbilê (English)

According to the book 'جبل الكرد' 'Mountain of the Kurds (Afrin)' Geographic Study:

Bilbil District (ناحية بلبل) consists of 48 administrative divisions and population centers, with Bilbil (بلبل) town as its center. Its boundaries: from the east Sheran District (ناحية شران), from the west Rajo District (ناحية راجو), from the south Mabetli District (ناحية معبطلي), and from the north the Turkish borders.

Bilbilê (بلبل), Bilbil (بلبل) /4683N – 640m/:

  • Bilbil (بلبل): A bird name that is abundant in the mountain forests around the village. Its name during the Ottoman period was 'Ezê' "عه زي" (Eh Zî), possibly derived from the name Izz al-Din (عزالدين).
  • Bilbil (بلبل) town is located at the end of the southern slope of the highest mountain in Aleppo Governorate (محافظة حلب), which is Girê Mezin (گرێ مەزن) with an elevation of 1269m. The town's elevation above sea level is 690m. Bilbil (بلبل) is about 50km from Afrin City (مدينة عفرين) and about 90km from Aleppo (حلب) - in the northwest direction. It is a small town, closer to being a large village, and has been a district center since the Ottoman period. It was the center of the Sheikh Ismail Zadeh (الشيخ إسماعيل زاده) family, and the town is located in the middle of the "Diban" (ديبان) tribal region. Its residents work in agriculture, especially olives, and some in livestock raising. It has some handicrafts such as agricultural machinery repair workshops, blacksmithing, some building materials, and some olive presses.

The site of Nabi Hori (نبي هوري) "the ancient city of Cyrus" (مدينة سيروس القديمة) is located about 12km to its east, which is a large and important archaeological site and a beautiful tourist location on the banks of the Afrin (عفرين) and Saboun (صابون) rivers.

In rainy years, dozens of springs explode around Bilbil (بلبل) town and its villages, turning them into lush gardens. The mountain elevations in Bilbil District (ناحية بلبل) contain iron and copper ore, and there are deposits of colored marble.

According to the book Afrin... Her River and Green Hills:

Bilbil (بلبل): A town in Kurdish Mountain (جبل الكرد), the center of a district, belonging to Afrin (منطقة عفرين), Aleppo Governorate (محافظة حلب).

It is located northwest of Afrin City (مدينة عفرين) on the lower southern slope with slight inclination of Mount Damarik (جبل داميريك), where the town overlooks the highest peak in Aleppo Governorate (محافظة حلب) (1269m). The mountain rocks are limestone and Cretaceous covered in most of their parts with green rocks and basalt. Its slopes with volcanic soil are suitable for agriculture and grazing due to the availability of water from springs or wells.

It is bordered to the north by a high mountain called Mount Damarik (جبل داميريك) (Girê Kabir گرێ کبیر) and Bali Koy (بالي كوي) and Za'reh (زعره) villages and the Turkish border directly, to the south by slopes and several stream paths and a high mountain chain and Ukanli (عوكانلي) village, to the west by mountain elevation and several stream paths and Heyamli (هياملي) and Baqjah Qonaq (باقجة قوناق) and Sa'riyanli (سعريانلي) villages, and to the east by mountain elevation planted with olive trees and Qurnah (قورنة) and Mahmoud Oba Si (محمود أوبه سي) villages.

A valley descends from the top of the mountain and passes through the center of the village heading south to Ukan Valley (وادي عوكان). The village has approximately 600 houses and is more than 700 years old. The old houses are stone and mud with flat wooden roofs, while the modern cement ones have intermixed with the old construction and extended southward, eastward, and westward. It has an electricity network, government departments, health center, telephone center, cultural center, and several elementary, preparatory, and secondary schools, police station, municipality building, district directorate building, bakery, and several shops for repairing agricultural machinery, blacksmithing, building materials, and some old and modern olive presses, pharmacies, and doctors.

The town depends on rainfed agriculture on 790 hectares for producing olives, grains, legumes, and vineyards, in addition to raising sheep and goats. Handicraft industries were also established, the most important being carpet making and building materials for consumption. The town drinks from an artesian well belonging to the state located south of the town. Roads to Bilbil (بلبل) villages and the region center are paved.

The town's name is new, and its old name from the Ottoman period was /Eh Zi/ (عه زي). Bilbil (بلبل) town was the center of the Sheikh Ismail Zadeh (الشيخ إسماعيل زاده) family and is located in the middle of the Diban (ديبان) tribal district. South of the town at 12km distance is the Nabi Hori (نبي هوري) archaeological site, formerly called "Cyrus" (سيروس) city, which is an archaeological, tourist, and beautiful site on the bank of Afrin (عفرين) and Saboun (صابون) rivers. Among its most important old families is the Sheikh Ismail Zadeh (الشيخ إسماعيل زاده) family, the first to settle there hundreds of years ago.

Bilbil (بلبل): A town in Kurdish Mountain (جبل الكرد), belonging to Bilbil District (ناحية بلبل), Afrin Region (منطقة عفرين), Aleppo Governorate (محافظة حلب).

It has been the district center since 1922, comprising 30 villages and 13 farms with a total area of 229.95 km2. It is bordered directly by the Turkish border to the north, by Sheran (شران) and Mabetli (معبطلي) districts to the south and east, and by Rajo (راجو) district to the west. Its elevation is 665m above sea level and it is about 50km from Afrin City (مدينة عفرين) and 90km from Aleppo (حلب) city. It is connected to its surroundings by main paved roads to Aleppo (حلب) and Afrin (عفرين) cities and neighboring villages. Bilbil District (ناحية بلبل) was established in 1922.

Village Head: Mohammed Colin Sido (محمد كولين سيدو)

II. Summary from Ax û Walat Transcript of Bilbilê

The village of Bilbilê, located in the Afrin region, holds a rich oral history regarding its origins and culture. Originally situated on a mountain known as Çiyayê Gir, which stands 1200 meters above sea level, the village was so secluded by dense forests that Ottoman tax collectors could not find it. According to local elders like Apê Hûrîk, the Ottomans were eventually guided to the location by the sound of a nightingale (bilbil), which led to the village being renamed "Bilbilê" and its inhabitants relocating to the mountain's base. The community was established by six founding families: Mala Cibqo (who were the first to settle), Mala Xûro, Mala Dêlî Hesen, Mala Dedo, Mala Qedîr Elî, and Mala Hecî Şêx Xelîl. Central to the village's life was the Qestel, a spring house with seven springs named after prominent figures like Esed, Otman, and Hecî Şêx Xelîl, whose waters irrigated the nearby Baxçê Bîstûnê (Garden of Bîstûn). The cultural life of Bilbilê is deeply rooted in communal activities, such as the preparation of traditional foods like Şişperik (dumplings in yogurt soup), Kutilk (kibbeh), and Qetmer (flatbread), often accompanied by the traditional art of Dengbêjî, with songs evoking pastoral life and historical pride, such as one referencing the significant landmark of Çiyayê Metîna (Metîna Mountain).

II. Summary from Ax û Walat Transcript of Bilbilê 2

The village of Bilbilê, part of a district in the Afrin region comprising 45 villages, holds a rich oral history and vibrant cultural traditions. According to a local elder named Hûrîk, the community was originally settled on a mountain known as Çiyayê Gêr by six large families from the Mazgînî tribe who had fled the Ottomans. These founding families were the Mala Jobko, Xurû, Del Hemlêro, Mala Dedo, Mala Hecî Şêx Xelîl, and Mala Qedîr Axa. The area was named Bilbilê because bulbul birds frequented its famous springs, particularly a site called Qestel, which featured seven springs that provided water for the entire village and its gardens. The community later relocated from the mountain to its present location following a conflict with an Agha from the nearby village of Bêkê, which resulted in the death of Hesikê Xurû of the Jobko clan. The cultural life of the village is demonstrated through the communal preparation of traditional foods, including Shishbarak (dumplings with meat and onion boiled in a yogurt sauce), Kutilk (kibbeh), Kitayî, and Nanê bi şîrînî (a sweet bread). This heritage is further preserved through the performance of traditional songs ("stranê kevin") by local musicians like Mihemed and Kemal, who sing of their love for the region's mountains accompanied by the saz.
