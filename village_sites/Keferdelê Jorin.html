I. Summary from TirejAfrin Site

According to the book 'جبل الكرد' 'Mountain of the Kurds' (Afrin) geographical study by Dr. Mohammed Abdo Ali:

Keferdelê Jorin, كفردلي فوقاني /1647N - 303E - 15km - 520m/:

  • كفردلي: The name is compound from كفر+ دلي. We could not determine the literal meaning of the name although "دلي" is a Turkish word meaning crazy or mad, and "كفر" is a Syriac-Greek term meaning farm.
  • A small village located on the edge of "وادي الجب" (Gelî Bîrê) valley descending steeply from north to south. Its location is strategic and overlooks it from its four directions calcareous mountain slopes. It is among the ancient villages in the region.

And according to the book: Afrin.... its river and green hills by writer Abdul Rahman Mohammed from Qatmah village:

كفردلي فوقاني: A village in Kurdish Mountain, belonging to the central district villages and Afrin region, Aleppo governorate.

It is a large village located on the western and southern slope of the edge of Al-Jub valley descending from north to south. Calcareous mountain slopes overlook it, carved by streams from the southern and eastern sides, and the village is located between two mountains in a deep depression. It is bordered to the north by a valley and mountain chain and Ghazi Tabah village. To the south by a valley and fertile plain of olive trees and Kafr Dali Tahtani village, to the west by a mountain chain and Satianli village, and to the east by a mountain chain planted with olive trees and slopes and Ma'rateh and Bablit villages. The number of houses reaches about 80 houses and its age is about 500 years. Its old houses are stone-mud with level wooden roofs while modern dwellings are of stone and cement. It has an electricity network and an elementary school modernized in 1947. Residents drink from artesian wells and water collects in pools. Its inhabitants work in olive, grain and legume cultivation and sheep and goat herding. The road reaching it is from the Al-Dariya junction to the village and is paved earth.

Among the families in the village: the Al-Bakr Agha family (Mohammed - Kinj) - the Al-Sha'a family - the Al-Taka family - the Al-Mamad family - the Al-Ali Issa family.

In the past Mr. Mohammed Abdullah, father of Dr. Khalil Abdullah, was mayor of this village, and currently Mr. Faiq Aibash is the village mayor.

There are many university degree holders and among the cultural and scientific figures in the village, to mention but not limit (Professor Azad Abdul Hanan / mathematics teacher known in the region - Professor Ahmed Kinj - poet Mohammed Sheikh Othman Hamdo (Dirsim Memo)).

Among the holders of higher degrees in the village: Abdul Rahman Khalil Aibash / doctorate in aviation mechanics / Russia

Village mayor: Faiq Aibash

II. Summary from Ax û Walat Transcript

The village school in Keferdelê Jorîn was established a long time ago (02:45), with an early prominent teacher in the Efrîn region being from this village (02:49). The first group of influential teachers in the Efrîn region included Heyder Gênco (from Keferdelê Jorîn), Xelîl Edîb (from Qujma), Beşîr Kedro (from Bênê), and Necah Silêman (from Hisê) (03:42-04:09). Mamosta Heyder Gênco founded the current school building in Keferdelê Jorîn in 1947, after teaching in homes for three years prior (04:16-04:32). The land for this school was donated by the village mukhtar, Mehmûdê Ebdela (04:40). Mamosta Heyder Gênco had received his baccalaureate in 1938 (05:25). When the school formally opened in 1947, it served children from seven surrounding villages (04:52, 10:18). This school is noted as the first of its kind in the villages of Efrîn, established in 1947 (10:04), with Heyder Qedûr and Xelîl Efendî named as its first teachers (10:14). The village has an old water well, estimated to be around 400 years old (16:39). Historically, during what is referred to as the "Mûrî" period, the large Gar cave was used to shelter the village's livestock (17:26). Another cave in the village served as a school in the 1900s and 1920s, where children were taught by Mullah Ebdilrehmanê Sultanî and Xoce Nebatî (17:33-17:41). Keferdelê Jorîn itself has a history spanning at least 400-500 years (18:19) and was initially settled by migrants (18:34). Before the establishment of permanent houses, inhabitants lived in caves (18:49-19:01). Keferdelê Jorîn is the older of two related villages; Keferdelê Jêrîn was originally a hamlet or cultivated area (mezra) associated with Keferdelê Jorîn (19:34-19:50). A significant cultural and historical event was the first widely celebrated Newroz in this Kurdish region, which was organized in Keferdelê Jorîn in 1961 (20:52). This occurred during the 1958-1961 "infîsal" (separation from the United Arab Republic) period (20:56-21:01). Key figures in organizing this Newroz included Hesen Hemdo Gênco, Ehmed Ebdulhenan, and Xelîl Ebdela (21:07-21:15). This era (1958-1961) was marked by the leadership of Syrian President Shukri al-Quwatli and Egyptian President Gamal Abdel Nasser, and the subsequent dissolution of their political union (21:31, 21:46-21:48). Other Syrian political figures from that general time mentioned include Mehmûd Elî Labêş and Edîb Şîşeklî (22:01-22:06). It's also mentioned that at one point, five Syrian presidents were of Kurdish origin (22:20). The Qedûr family, one of the four main families in Keferdelê (others being Elî, Teka, and Şaha (22:28)), traces its lineage to the Aşîtî tribe from Kaniya Sipî in the Wan region (25:51-25:54). The village also remembers three individuals, Textor Ferzende, Demhat, and Serxwebûn, as martyrs who died in the national liberation war (26:33). Old wedding traditions in the village included celebrations that lasted for a week, with brides arriving on horseback (33:54-34:03). In contemporary times, education in the region's schools is conducted in the Kurdish language (07:47-07:50).
