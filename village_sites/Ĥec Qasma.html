I. Summary from TirejAfrin Site

According to the book 'جبل الكرد' 'Mountain of the Kurds' (Afrin City) Geographical Study by Dr. Mohammed Abdo Ali (الدكتور محمد عبدو علي):

Ḧec Qasma, حاج قاسملي, حاج قاسم /574n - 277ha - 15km - 600m/:

  • Qasim (قاسم): A local proper name of its first inhabitant, and "Haj" (حج) is his religious title.
  • It is located on the southern slope of a plateau overlooking Khastiya Valley (وادي خاستيا), with oak forests, pastures, and olive groves spread on it.

According to the book 'عفرين .... نهرها وروابيها الخضراء' 'Afrin City.... Its Rivers and Green Hills' by Abdulrahman Mohammed from Qatmah village (عبدالرحمن محمد من قرية قطمه):

حاج قاسملي: A village in Mountain of the Kurds (جبل الكرد), belonging to Mabetli district (ناحية المعبطلي), Afrin City region (منطقة عفرين), Aleppo governorate (محافظة حلب).

It is a small village located in the middle part of the aforementioned mountain, on the southern slope of a limestone plateau sloping toward the southeast, cut by streams with oak forests and pastures spread on it. It is 15km from Mabetli town (بلدة المعبطلي) toward the southwest. Its soil is hilly suffering from erosion.

It is bounded to the north by a high mountain chain planted with forest trees and Tor Mishkanli (طور مشكانلي) and Magharjiq (مغارجق) villages, to the south by a mountain slope and Sili Valley (وادي سيلي) and Jarjam Valley (وادي جرجم) and Rutanli (روطانلي) and Iki Akhur (إيكي آخور) villages, to the west by a slope and Sili Valley (وادي سيلي) and mountain chain and Jarjam Valley (وادي جرجم) and Dalyanli (داليانلي) village, and to the east by mountain elevations and Sili Valley (وادي سيلي) and Mastu Ashur (مستو عاشور) village.

The number of its houses reaches 50 houses and its age is about 250 years. Its dwellings are stone and clay with flat wooden roofs, while the modern ones are of stone and concrete spread on the outskirts of the village.

It has an electricity network and elementary school. The village drinks from cisterns that collect rainwater in winter or from artesian wells dug next to houses, and currently from a government well south of the village.

Its residents work in rain-fed agriculture on an area of 277 hectares with olive trees and grains, in addition to raising sheep, goats, and cattle. It connects with the district center and neighboring villages by a paved road to the village center.

Among its most important families: (Haj Qasim family (عائلة حاج قاسم) - and Mish Buk family (عائلة مش بوك)).

Village mayor: Zahed Ikhtiyar (زاهد اختيار)

II. Summary from Ax û Walat Transcript

The village Hec Qasma belongs to the Bîya tribe (2:34), originally from Dêr Sowanê (2:39). They migrated via Bînol, Bêdîna (2:41), and Sîn Gonkê (2:44) before moving from the Or area (2:56) towards the Çiyayê Xastiyan region (3:30). The village is approximately 750 years old (5:07), with concentrated settlement in the last 450 years (5:11). Historically, mountains were preferred for settlement (5:15), with many mountain villages founded in the 20th century (5:18). The founder, Heciyê Qasim (10:27), moved from Sîngonkê in the 17th century (10:33) to Çiyayê Xastiyan for charcoal/wood production (10:39), establishing the village (10:51). Hec Qasma is considered the oldest village in the area (11:16). Early families included arrivals from Qermîliqê (7:16) and others around 200-300 years ago (7:24). Some ancestors came from Mosul, Iraq (31:17, 31:29), arriving during the Ottoman mobilization ("seferberlîkê") (31:47). The historical economy depended on forest products like charcoal sold in Aleppo (4:17, 8:46), later shifting to grapes (8:59) and olives (9:02). Animal transport necessitated traditional crafts like saddle-making (7:40, 30:43), passed down generations (31:09). Settlements were historically near springs (8:14). An elderly resident recalls making charcoal and travelling under a French mandate license ("destûrê firengî") for 35 years (20:38, 23:05). A secret language has been spoken for generations (12:14), partly for security (12:17). Notable past figures include Muxtar Heydo (~40 years service) (38:39), Ezîzê Omê (38:51), Mistê Bozo (38:58), and anti-French fighters Henanê Mîrê and Ebdê Ehoş (39:04). Teacher Mihemed Heso aided development before his death in 2014 (39:18). Many traditional games (41:47) declined with migration and television (42:30), and past life is remembered as more communal (43:06).
