Basic Information about Gewrika

Source: Tirej Afrin

Etymology: The name is a diminutive of 'white' in Kurdish (gewr + ik). However, the correct meaning in local Kurdish refers to an open space free of trees in a forested land, which matches the Arabic translation 'Al-Fusha' (The Clearing).

Foundation Date/Period: Approximately 500 years ago

Hills: Meydan

Summaries

I. Summary from TirejAfrin Site (English) of Gewrika

Source: https://www.tirejafrin.com/site/kura%20afrin%20Cindires%20-%20Gewrika.htm

According to the book جبل الكرد (عفرين) دراسة جغرافية Çiyayê Kurmênc (Efrîn): A Geographical Study by د. محمد عبدو علي Dr. Mihemed Ebdo Elî, the village is named Gewrika, Kawarkan, or Al Fusha. The entry lists the population as 2325 people, the area as 700 hectares, the distance as 15 kilometers, and the elevation as 470 meters.

Regarding the etymology, Gewrik is the diminutive of white in Kurdish. Gewrik is also the name of a Kurdish tribe in Eastern Kurdistan near Mahabad. However, the correct derivation for this location is that Gewrik in local Kurdish means an open clearing free of trees within a forested land. The site of the village was historically empty of trees, from which the precise Arabic translation of the Kurdish word "Al Fusha" (The Clearing) was derived.

It is a large village located on a high slope, divided into two halves by the village valley. At the top of the village, there is a water spring that used to irrigate the homes. Its site is archaeological, containing many caves and grottoes that were inhabited in ancient times. It is considered one of the prosperous villages.

According to the book عفرين .... نهرها وروابيها الخضراء Efrîn... Her River and Her Green Hills by عبدالرحمن محمد Ebdulrehman Mihemed from the village of Qetme, Gewrika is a village in Çiyayê Kurmênc following the Cindirês district in the Efrîn region, Heleb governorate. It is a large village situated in mountainous land where several water streams gather and flow downward toward the south. It is surrounded by several heights, including the Meydan mountain which is 650 meters high. Its soil is clay and fertile alluvial. It is located 15 kilometers from the town of Cindirês toward the northeast.

The village is bordered to the north by mountainous heights planted with olive trees, vines, and forest trees, as well as the village of Satiyanlî. To the south, it is bordered by a slope, a stream, and the village of Qerebaş at a distance of 500 meters. To the east, it is bordered by the Riya Îkî Axûr (Road to Îkî Axûr), a stream, a mountainous height planted with olive trees, and the village of Keferdelê Jêrîn. To the west, it is bordered by high mountainous heights planted with olive trees and the village of Colaqan.

The number of houses reaches approximately 150, and the village is about 500 years old according to the statements of one of the residents. It is one of the old villages in the region. In 1925, it served as the temporary headquarters for the Kurdax region for a short period during the French Mandate before the administration moved to the current site of Efrîn city.

Its old houses are made of stone and mud with flat wooden roofs, while the modern ones are cement. The village has expanded to the east, south, and west. A stream passes through the center of the village, starting from the north and ending in the south of the village. The village has an electricity network, a primary school, and a middle school. A municipality center was established in 1988, which includes the neighboring villages of Qerebaş, Colaqan, and Xerzan. It contains an old mosque in the center of the village, an agricultural cooperative society, a telephone line, and a modern olive press.

The village draws drinking water from a network connected to an artesian well dug southeast of the village with a depth of 120 meters. Most residents work in the cultivation of olives, grains, and legumes via rainfed farming (700 hectares), and in raising sheep and goats. They also cultivate summer vegetables, walnuts, apricots, and pomegranates via irrigation from artesian wells. The village is considered beautiful due to its construction from beautiful hewn stone. It is connected to the district center by a paved road.

Among the holders of higher degrees in the village are Reşîd Tatûz (PhD in Agriculture from Bulgaria), Mistefa Beşîr (PhD in Economics from Russia), and Dr. Ehmed Şewqî (PhD in Journalism from Bulgaria). It is noted that the photographer Ebdo Lîlav and the plastic artist Şukran Bîlal are children of this village.

The mukhtar of the village is Mistefa Şewket.
