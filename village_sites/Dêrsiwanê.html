I. Summary from TirejAfrin Site of Dêrsiwanê (English)

According to the book جبل الكرد|Mountain of the Kurds (Afrin) Geographic Study:

Dêrsiwan (ديرسوان) /3802N - 2090E - 12km - 480m/:

  • A name for a monastery and an ancient archaeological site.
  • Deir Sawan (ديرسوان): A large village. To its west and about 2km away are two bridges from the Roman era, which still serve local transportation to this day. It is an important center for the Haj Omar (حج أومر) family known in the history of Jabal al-Kurd (جبل الكرد) in ancient times, and for the family of Sheikh Ismail Zadeh (شيخ إسماعيل زاده) as well.

According to the book عفرين.... نهرها ورواييها الخضراء|Afrin.... Her River and Green Hills:

Deir Sawan: A village in Jabal al-Kurd (جبل الكرد) belonging to Sharran District (ناحية شران), Afrin Region (منطقة عفرين), Aleppo Governorate (محافظة حلب), (3673 N).

It is a large village located on a limestone plateau with white clayey soil about 11km from the town of Sheran (شران) in the northeast direction. It is bounded to the north by slope and valley, the course of the Afrin River (نهر عفرين) and Saboun Sih (الصابون سيه) and the village of Sheltah (شلتاح). To the south by valley and plain and the village of Wira Kan (ويره كان). To the east by mountain chain planted with olive and forest trees and the village of Iki Dam (إيكي دام). To the west by valley and plain and the course of the Afrin River (نهر عفرين) and the village of Zaytunak (زيتوناك). The number of its houses is about 300 houses and the village age is about 400 years. It has a town hall and electricity network and drinking water from artesian wells and water collection pools. It has an elementary school and a health center and a mosque and a police station and a town hall recently established. Its old dwellings are stone of clay and wooden roofs and the modern ones are of stone and cement. The paved road passes through its center connecting it to the neighboring villages up to Nabi Houri castle (قلعة النبي هوري) and the Roman archaeological bridge which is about 1km from Deir Sawan (دير صوان). The village residents work in olive, grape and grain cultivation and other fruit trees. There are two modern olive presses in the village. As for the Afrin River (نهر عفرين), it passes north of Deir Sawan (دير صوان) under an ancient Roman bridge 92 meters long, passing under a second Roman bridge north of the town of Deir Sawan (دير صوان) and the two branches join west of the town of Deir Sawan (دير صوان) about 2km in the main course of the Afrin River (نهر عفرين) and the two bridges are carried on seven ancient columns and this bridge is narrow allowing only one car to pass. Among its most important families is the family of Faiq Sheikh Ismail Zadeh (فايق شيخ اسماعيل زاده) and it is one of the ancient villages in Sheran district (ناحية شران).

It is mentioned that the late Adnan Dalbergen (عدنان دلبرين) is one of the sons of this village.

Village mayor: Hamo Hanan Hussein (حمو حنان حسين)

II. Summary from Ax û Walat Transcript of Dêrsiwanê

The village of Dêrsiwanê, a community of approximately four hundred households located in the Shera district of Afrin, is historically significant as the home of the renowned and influential artist Adnan Dilbirîn. Remembered as a pioneer, Adnan was celebrated for creating a unique musical rhythm and style that was rare in the Afrin region and inspired other artists who followed him. His life was deeply intertwined with his village, where he was born and later married in his family's home. The narrative of his life includes serving two and a half years in the military and the profound impact of his mother's 20-year-long illness. A central theme of his story is his deep attachment to his homeland; despite temporarily relocating to Aleppo and acquiring a house, shop, and car, he and his family chose to return to Dêrsiwanê out of love for their village. Adnan Dilbirîn passed away after a final illness lasting twenty days, and his death was mourned by the community, with his friends organizing a memorial gathering at the local mosque. His legacy is preserved and commemorated by cultural organizations such as the "Culture and Art of Colorful Afrin," which frames his life and work within the broader context of Kurdish heritage.

II. Summary from Ax û Walat Transcript of Dêrsiwanê 2

Gundê Dêrsiwanê is one of the largest villages in the Shera district of Afrin, founded over 400 years ago by Kurdish citizens and now home to approximately 4,000 people in 400 houses. According to village elders, the settlement was originally located on a nearby hill and named Sîwan, but was relocated to its current position by the river due to a lack of water, at which point it was renamed Dêrsiwan. The old site was later excavated by Turks who discovered jars of fuel, and the area was also bombed during the French Mandate. The village's historical landscape is defined by its proximity to Kela Hûrî (Cyrrhus Castle) and two ancient Roman bridges that cross the Afrin River. This river is formed by the confluence of two water sources originating in Northern Kurdistan, one of which is known as Kaniya Germikê near Dîlok (Gaziantep), which merge about a kilometer above the Meydankê Dam. Dersîwan is most famously the home of the immortal Kurdish artist Adnan Dilbirîn, who is buried in the village cemetery after his death in a traffic accident in 1999. He is remembered for having a profound influence on Kurdish music across all four regions and for introducing a new, modern style that moved beyond the traditional slow folk dances of Afrin. The village's cultural life continues through groups like the folkloric dance troupe Koma Şehîd Çekdar, which was founded in 2011.
