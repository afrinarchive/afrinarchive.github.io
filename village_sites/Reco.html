I. Summary from TirejAfrin Site of Reco (English)

Villages of Rajo (راجو) District / Reco

Rajo (راجو) - Reco

According to the book 'جبل الكرد' 'Mountain of the Kurds' (Afrin City) Geographical Study by Dr. Mohammed Abdo Ali (الدكتور محمد عبدو علي):

Rajo District (ناحية راجو) is one of the old districts in the Afrin (عفرين) region, with its center being Rajo (راجو) town. It includes 65 administrative divisions and population settlements, seven of which are abandoned. District borders: from the west and north Turkey (تركيا), from the southeast Ma'batli (معبطلي) district and from the southwest Sheikh al-Hadid (شيخ الحديد) district.

Reco (راجو), Rajo (راجو) /4110n - 565m/:

  • The original name is Reca (رجا) Reca, which is a Kurdish proper name sometimes derived from "Rajab" (رجب), and the letter O from Reco is a masculine calling tool.
  • Rajo (راجو) town is located on an elevation in the middle of the "Baliya" (باليا) plain, its houses slope gently toward the west and south. It is 25km from Afrin City (عفرين) - in the northwestern direction. Most of its inhabitants work in olive cultivation, they also grow grains and some types of fruit trees in small fields. In the town there are commercial shops and workshops for repairing machinery and blacksmithing and carpentry, and its weekly market "Bazar" (بازار) on Saturday. The Rajo (راجو) - Meydan Ikbis (ميدان أكبس) railway line passes through it, and it has an important train station dating back to the Ottoman era. Its location is beautiful and surrounded by forest mountains from all directions. Villages and farms belong to it.

According to the book 'عفرين.... نهرها ورواباتيها الخضراء' 'Afrin... Its River and Green Hills' by writer Abdulrahman Mohammed (عبدالرحمن محمد) from Qatma (قطمه) village:

Rajo District (ناحية راجو): A district in the Kurdish Mountain (جبل الكرد), belonging to Afrin (عفرين) region, Aleppo (حلب) governorate.

The district is located in the northern part of the Kurdish Mountain (جبل الكرد). It is bordered to the north by Turkey (تركيا), to the east by Bilbil (بلبل) and Sheran (شران) districts, to the south by Ma'batli (معبطلي) and Sheikh Hadid (شيخ حديد) districts, and to the west by Turkey (تركيا) and Alexandretta (اسكندرون) district. The town of Rajo (راجو) includes 45 villages and twenty farms.

Rajo Town (بلدة راجو): A town and district center in the Kurdish Mountain (جبل الكرد), belonging to Afrin (عفرين) region, Aleppo (حلب) governorate.

Rajo (راجو) town is located on the peak of a limestone elevation 585m high and the town slopes gently westward toward the Baliya (باليا) plain. Its soil is red and fertile. It is 25 km from Afrin City (عفرين) in the northwestern direction. It is bordered to the north by a wide plain planted with olive trees and Mamali (مامالي) and Maskanli (مسكانلي) villages. To the south by a wide fertile plain and high mountain chain and several villages neighboring the town: Darwish (درويش) village and Oba Si (أوبه سي) and Atmanli (عطمانلي) and Musik Oba Si (موسيك أوبه سي), to the east by a slope and Haj Khalil (حاج خليل) village close to it 1 km away, and to the west overlooks a flat fertile land and the old and archaeological train station from the Ottoman era in 1911 and Hobkanli (هوبكانلي) and Banik (بانيك) villages.

Rajo (راجو) is divided into two parts:

The first part: very old located on an elevation from the eastern side of the town.

The second part: the modern part on the plain from the western and southern sides.

The number of houses is approximately 1200 and its age is about 400 years. Its old dwellings are stone and clay with wooden roofs. Most of them have been demolished due to the spread of modern construction that has intermingled with the old construction and expanded on all sides of the town. It has an electricity network and water network connected to artesian wells 1 km south of it. It also benefits from household cisterns that store rainwater.

It has a center for the town council and health care clinic and cultural center and telephone center and police station and building for the district directorate as it is a center for the district directorate and primary, preparatory and secondary schools and town hall and meteorological station and train station. There are also several commercial and service shops on both sides of the main road. It has several modern olive oil presses.

The population works in rainfed agriculture and produces olives, grains, legumes and grapes on an area of 630 hectares and irrigated agriculture from artesian wells for vegetables and fruit trees on a small area of 5 hectares and raising sheep and goats due to the availability of pastures on limestone slopes. Iron hematite ore deposits have been discovered in the region at Alamdar (علمدار) village which is currently used in iron cement manufacturing.

The district connects to the region by an asphalted road passing through its center to Meydan Ikbis (ميدان إكبس) and the Turkish border, and the other road passes from the center eastward to Bilbil (بلبل) and Sheran (شران) districts. The railway line passes on the western side of the town coming from Aleppo (حلب) city passing through Rajo (راجو) station to the Syrian-Turkish border Meydan Ikbis (ميدان إكبس) station for trains.

A market is held every Saturday and dates back to the early thirties of the twentieth century. It is one of the old districts in the Afrin (عفرين) region, established in 1922, and the population of Rajo (راجو) town is 4040 people and the total population of Rajo (راجو) district is 70048 people.

Village chief: Mr. Arif Ibrahim (عارف ابراهيم)
