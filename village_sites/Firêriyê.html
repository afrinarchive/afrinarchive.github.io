Possible Village Name Meaning of Firêriyê

Believed to be composed of the Kurdish words for spacious, Fere (فره), and road, Rê (ره), meaning "the wide path" (الدرب الواسع).

Summaries

I. Summary from TirejAfrin Site of Firêriyê (English)

According to the book جبل الكرد|Mountain of the Kurds (Afrin) Geographic Study by Dr. Mohammed Abdo Ali:

Firêriyê (فريرية) /1554n - 13075h - 12km - 195m/:

  • I believe the name is composed of: spacious Fere (فره), and road Rê (ره), meaning "the wide path" (الدرب الواسع).
  • A large village 2 km from the Afrin River (نهر عفرين). It has beautiful villas for the sons of Al Ammu family (آل عمو) and is considered a main center for them.

According to the book: Afrin... Her River and Green Hills by writer Abdulrahman Mohammed from Qatmeh village:

Firêriyê (فريرية): A village in the Kurdish Mountain (جبل الكرد), belonging to Jindires District (جندريس), Afrin (عفرين), Aleppo Governorate (حلب).

It is a large village located in the eastern section of the Jindires plain, 2 km north of the Afrin River valley (وادي نهر عفرين). It is 12 km east of Jindires town. Its soil is alluvial and fertile, rich in groundwater. It is bordered to the north by a fertile plain planted with olive trees and Tal Hamo village (تل حمو), and to the south by a fertile agricultural plain planted with olive trees and the course of the Afrin River valley (وادي نهر عفرين) and Jalmah village (جلمة) at the far south with the borders of Idlib Governorate (إدلب) and Samaan Mountain (جبل سمعان), and to the west by a fertile agricultural plain and Al-Ramadiya village (الرمادية), and to the east by a fertile plain planted with olive trees and Kefar Zit village (كفر زيت) on a high mountain elevation. The number of its houses is about 135 houses and the village's age is about 200 years according to one of the village residents. The first to settle in the village was the Al Ammu family (آل عمو), and Al Ammu is considered among the founders of the village. Its old houses are clay with roofs of branches and straw on two opposite slopes, and the modern ones are stone and concrete. Currently, there are two magnificent villas on the northern side of the village for Dr. Omar Al Ammu (الدكتور عمر آل عمو) and his family. It has electricity network. The village drinks from a water network connected to artesian wells in the neighboring Ghazawiya village (الغزاوية). It has elementary and preparatory schools, a mosque, a telephone center, an agricultural association, a party branch, and a municipality established two years ago. The road from it to Jindires and neighboring villages is paved. Two farms follow it (eastern Zallaqa and western Zallaqa) (زلاقة شرقي وزلاقة غربي). Most of its residents work in growing olives, grains, and legumes through dry farming (1075 hectares) and irrigation (300 hectares) by pumping from the Afrin River (نهر عفرين) for growing sugar beet, pomegranates, and summer vegetables, in addition to raising sheep and cattle. One of its most important families is: the Al Ammu family (آل عمو).

Village mayor: Omar Faidi Al Ammu (عمر فيضي آل عمو)
