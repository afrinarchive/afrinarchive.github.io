II. Summary of Kitakh from Khalil Sino

Source: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fBX558HB9pg

The documentary episode broadcasted by the channel [Hatim Li Efrînê] takes place in the picturesque springtime setting of the [Qetax] plain in the [Efrîn] region. Rather than focusing on the historical foundation or tribal lineages of a single village, the hosts use this natural backdrop to interview an eight member youth folklore and musical group known as [Êldaf]. The group performs traditional music at weddings and local celebrations, aiming to preserve and revitalize the broader cultural heritage of the region.

The band consists of young men and women from various districts and villages across [Efrîn]. Members introduced in the transcript include [Ehmed], [Hemîde], [Dijla Bozan], [Silêman Mihemed], and [Ciwan Bozan], all of whom hail from the [Cindirêsê] district. In addition, [Axîn Reşo] represents the village of [Qurtqulaqa] in the [Bilbilê] district, and [Roza Dawid] comes from the [Mabeta] district.

The members of [Êldaf] explain that their artistic project began a few months prior to the interview. The musicians observed that traditional wedding celebrations in [Efrîn] had shifted away from their ancient roots, which once heavily featured rhythmic instruments like the def and zurne. By reintroducing these acoustic folk elements, they hope to bring a fresh yet historically grounded atmosphere to modern celebrations. [Roza Dawid] adds that their vision is not limited to local traditions; they seek to incorporate elements from all four parts of [Kurdistanê] into their performances. [Ehmed] notes that their foundational skills come from older folk dance instructors, some of whom still reside in [Efrîn] while others have moved to [Heleb].

[Axîn Reşo] notes that when they first performed with traditional dress and instruments, the public was pleasantly surprised. She explains that the women in the group plan to soon wear the authentic three piece dresses known as sêcef, which were traditionally worn by older generations of women in [Efrîn]. [Silêman Mihemed] emphasizes that their primary goal is not financial gain. While they accept small compensations to cover their travel and logistical expenses, their core mission is to elevate the indigenous culture and folklore of [Efrîn].

Throughout the episode, the group performs several traditional songs accompanied by rhythmic beats. The lyrics of these songs reference the beauty of [Efrîn], the agricultural life of the villagers, and local geographic features such as the [Cûmê] plain. Through their art, the youth of [Êldaf] actively work to ensure that the rich artistic legacy of their ancestors continues to thrive among the new generations in the region.

The featured guests are three young men who perform both individually and as

of artists, singers, and instrumentalists. He recalls his first public

performance at the age of eight during a family celebration.

The second guest is Menanê Şah, a musician from the village of Qirtme who is

twenty one years old. The third is Memcan Çîlko, a student of English

literature from the village of Çeqelê Cûmê who is twenty years old. Together,

these young men use their academic backgrounds in music and their deep

traditional roots to perform at festivals and events, ensuring that the

folkloric songs of Efrînê are not lost, especially among those who migrated to

Helebê.

During the interview, the hosts and guests discuss the importance of traditional

music. The host Xelîl expresses great pride in seeing the youth continue the

legacy of legendary local singers such as Emîd, Ednan Dilbirîn, and [Elî

Tico]. The young men share stories of how they gained recognition. Candar

mentions that a video of them singing a mix of local folklore, including the

song Narînê, out in the snow unexpectedly went viral, bringing them widespread

attention.

The transmission of musical knowledge is also highlighted. One of the guests

credits his uncle, the esteemed musician Hesen Mihemed, for teaching him how

to play the tembur and instilling in him a profound understanding of authentic

local melodies. The episode is filled with live musical performances, where the

young men sing various traditional songs and ballads, showcasing their vocal

talents.

At the conclusion of the video, the host Xelîl thanks the musicians, the

channel Hatim Li Efrînê, the cameraman Hoşyar Heyder, and a man named Elî

who helped organize the gathering space. Ultimately, while the transcript lacks

data on ancient village origins, shrines, or caves, it serves as a vivid

cultural document illustrating how the youth from villages like Xesê,

Qirtme, and Çeqelê Cûmê are actively preserving the artistic spirit and

social bonds of the Efrînê region.
