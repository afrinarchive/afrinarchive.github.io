I. Summary from TirejAfrin Site of Şîtka (English)

According to the book 'جبل الكرد' 'Mountain of the Kurds' (عفرين) Geographic Study by Dr. Mohammed Abdo Ali:

Šîtka (شيركانلي), Shirkanli (شيركانلي), Shirkan (شيركان) /1378n - 314ha - 7km - 640m/:

  • Shitka (شيتكا): In Kurdish meaning the active and energetic / Kurani Dictionary (قاموس كوراني). The original name is Jîrka (جيركا) "the active ones", and the letter J was replaced with Š as is common among the Kurds.
  • A medium village located on the western slope of a limestone hill.

According to the book: Afrin... Its River and Green Hills by writer Abdul Rahman Mohammed (عبدالرحمن محمد) from Qatmah village (قرية قطمه):

Shirkanli (شيركانلي): A village in Mountain of the Kurds (جبل الكرد), belonging to Mabetli District (ناحية المعبطلي), Afrin City region (منطقة عفرين), Aleppo Governorate (محافظة حلب).

It is a large village located in the central part of Mountain of the Kurds (جبل الكرد), on the western slope of a limestone hill crossed by streams flowing toward the south and west. It is 7km away from Mabetli town toward the southwest. Its soil is fertile clay. It is bounded to the north by a slope and Sili valley (وادي سيلي) and Mirkanli village (قرية ميركانلي) nearby at a distance of 1km, and to the south by a slope and several streams and mountain elevations planted with olive trees and forest trees and Iki Akhur village (قرية إيكي آخور), and to the west by a slope and Sili valley (وادي سيلي) and Jarjam valley (وادي جرجم) planted with walnut and apricot trees and a mountain elevation and Musto Ashur village (قرية مستو عاشور), and to the east by a slope and several streams and mountain elevations and Darker Kabir village (قرية داركير كبير). The number of its houses is about 100 houses and its population is about 500 people. It is one of the old villages in Mabetli District (ناحية معبطلي) and Afrin City region (منطقة عفرين). Its dwellings are stone-clay with flat wooden roofs. It has developed architecturally and modern cement houses have dominated it. There are several magnificent villas on the western side of the village. It has an electricity network, elementary school, mosque, telephone center, and agricultural association. The village drinks from wells and cisterns that collect rainwater. Its inhabitants cultivate rain-fed crops on an area of 244 hectares with grains, legumes, and olive trees, and they cultivate irrigated crops on an area of 70 hectares in the south of the village (sugar beet, summer vegetables, walnuts, and almonds), in addition to raising sheep and goats. There is an olive press in it. It is connected to the district center by a paved road to the center of the village. Its most important families are (Faqih family) (فقه).

Village mayor: Kamal Mohammed Hamo (كمال محمد حمو)

II. Summary from Ax û Walat Transcript of Şîtka

The village of Şitka, located in the Mabeta region of Afrin between the villages of Hisên and Kaxrê, is a community of approximately eighty houses built upon ancient ruins. Its history began with the settlement of two families, the Kurmûne family from the Zerwar tribe of Kobanî and the Evdê Aşêre family, later joined by others including the Şêx Mislim family from the Beraziyê region. The village developed a strong tradition of self-governance without feudal lords ("axa"), priding itself on its unity and self-sufficiency. This was expressed through its rich cultural life, which included hosting bards (Dengbêj) like Hesen Nazî and fostering local talent such as the illiterate poet and actor Reşîdê Elî, whose socially critical works were transcribed by his brother Mecîd. The villagers transformed their mountainous landscape, particularly since the 1970s, by clearing land to plant olives, grapes, sumac, and various fruit trees, and digging wells when their natural springs dried up. This agricultural life supported the annual tradition of preparing "zexîre" (winter provisions), including grape molasses (dims), fruit leather (bastiq), strained yogurt (mastê kemandî), and a savory thyme mix (zehter). This legacy of resilience and cultural identity continues into the present, as the village honors its twenty martyrs and its children recite patriotic poems about Rojava and the YPJ, taught to them by their parents.

II. Summary from Ax û Walat Transcript of Şîtka 2

The village of Şitka, an old and self-reliant community of approximately eighty households, is situated in the mountainous Mabeta region between the villages of Gundê Hisên and Kaxrê. According to its elders, the village was founded on previously uncultivated land by ancestors who migrated from the Kobanî area, specifically from Til Hecib. The initial settlement began with two families, Malê Kurmanc of the Zerwan tribe and Malê Ebdê Aşê[[,]] and was later expanded by other families, including some from the Berazî tribe. Historically, the village was known for its prominent households ("şikat") and its independence, having never been under the rule of a feudal lord ("axa"). This strong identity is also defined by sacrifice, as the community has given nineteen to twenty martyrs ("pakrewan"). The cultural heritage of Şitka includes a rich oral tradition, exemplified by the local illiterate poet and artist Reşîdê Elî, and was a place where traveling bards (dengbêj) like Hesenê Nazê would perform. This culture is deeply tied to the land and its agricultural cycle, with villagers engaging in the extensive preparation of traditional winter provisions ("zexîre"), including grape molasses (dibs), fruit leather (bastiq), stuffed eggplant (meqdûs), strained yogurt (mastê kemandî), and a variety of dried vegetables, all made using traditional methods passed down through generations.

II. Summary from Ax û Walat Transcript of Şîtka 3

Şitka is an old village in the Mabeta district of Afrin, situated between the villages of Hisên and Kaxrê. According to a village elder, its history began with just two founding families, the Kurmûne family (from the Zerwaran tribe of Kobanî) and the Avdê Aşêne family, with early inhabitants living in caves before houses were built. The community grew over time with the arrival of other families, such as Hemû, Qolaxê, and Şêx Mislim. The village's livelihood, rooted in a strong tradition of self-sufficiency, has been based on olive cultivation since the 1970s, a shift from a more diverse agriculture of the past. This self-reliance is also evident in their history of hand-digging deep wells for water when natural springs proved unreliable centuries ago. This tradition extends to their cultural practices, especially the preparation of "zexîre" (winter provisions), which includes a variety of preserved foods like grape molasses, fig leather, pepper paste, and a unique preserved yogurt known as "mastê kemandî." Culturally, the village is home to the "Şehîd Viyan" theatre group, which for 35 years has created plays, songs, and dances that reflect and critique the realities of their community. The village, which consists of eighty households, has also given nineteen martyrs.

III. Summary from other Channels' Transcripts of Şîtka

A. Summary from Halil Sino Transcript of Şîtka

The village of Şitka, estimated to be 300-400 years old, traces its origins to two founding brothers, Şêrko and Mîrko, who also established the neighboring village of Hisê. This close historical bond is further evidenced by their shared cemetery, which serves both communities. Historically, life in Şitka was deeply rooted in agriculture, a past represented by traditional stone olive presses (dalîb) and an ancient oak tree believed to be as old as the village itself. The community's cultural and religious life centered around sites like the Shrine of Sheikh Hamid, where traditional practices of making vows and sacrifices have since ceased, and the Salah al-Din Mosque, which was constructed in 1984. In the modern era, the village has experienced significant changes, including the disappearance of its local market and the emigration of many residents to places like Lebanon, Europe, and Germany, a connection highlighted by a greeting to a former teacher, Aziz Muhammad, now living abroad. The agricultural economy remains vulnerable, as seen in the recent failure of the apricot harvest due to frost, yet aspects of traditional rural life, such as commerce conducted by itinerant vendors, continue to be a part of the village's daily fabric.

C. Summary from Afrin 366 Transcript of Şîtka

The village of Şitko, originally known as Şitûbî, was founded by the Sheikhan tribe on a site of old ruins. Its name is derived from a tradition of hospitality where the tribe's Sheikh would provide milk to travelers, who would ask "şit ko?" ("where is the milk?"). The current inhabitants identify as Kurmanj and trace their lineage to "Ezîz Emê Xozê," considering themselves the original families of the village. An example of the village's historical construction is an old stone house featuring a plaque dated to 1361 Hijri (approximately 1942 AD), which was built by Turkish masons who came from Lebanon. The plaque itself is inscribed in Arabic script but reflects a Turkish style and language.
