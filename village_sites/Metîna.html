I. Summary from TirejAfrin Site of Metîna (English)

According to the book 'جبل الكرد' 'Mountain of the Kurds' / عفرين / Geographic Study:

Metîna, متينلي (متينلي), Al-Daha (الضحى) /1962n - 405h - 3km - 530m/:

  • Metina (متينا): The name of a mountain and known region in southern Kurdistan (كردستان). "Metinan" (متينان) is one of the Kurdish tribal clans, /Wasfi Zakaria p.664/.
  • A large village located on the western slope of a limestone plateau.

According to the book 'عفرين .... نهرها ورواببيها الخضراء' 'Afrin.... Its River and Green Hills':

ماتنلي (ماتنلي): A village located in Kurdish Mountain (جبل الكرد) belonging to Sheran district (ناحية شران), Afrin region (منطقة عفرين), Aleppo governorate (محافظة حلب).

It is a large village located on an elevation and mountain slope in the northern part of Sam'an Mountain (جبل سمعان) on a limestone plateau. A seasonal stream passes through it flowing toward the Afrin River (نهر عفرين). It is connected by limestone vegetation. It is bordered to the north by a wide plain planted with olive and vineyard trees and Khirbet Sheran village (قرية خربة شران). To the south are a deep valley and mountain chains and valleys and Mash'ala village (قرية مشعلة). To the east lie high mountain chains planted with olive, vineyard, and forest trees and Qatma village (قرية قاطمة). To the west are slopes and fertile agricultural plains and the Bulbul and Medanaki road (طريق بلبل وميدانكي) and Halabi Kabir village (قرية حلبي كبير).

The number of its houses is about 100 houses and its age is about 400 years. Its old houses are clay-stone with wooden roofs, and the modern ones are cement. It spread in the northern and southwestern directions. It has an electrical network and drinking water belonging to the state as well as telephone, mosque, and elementary school. A paved road connects it to the district and region, and it belongs to Sheran municipality (بلدية شران). Currently, a large water tank is being built on the western side of the village to distribute water from Medanaki Dam (سد ميدانكي) for drinking to the cities of Afrin City (عفرين) and A'zaz (أعزاز).

The village residents work in rainfed agriculture, olives, and irrigated grains from artesian wells (vegetables - pomegranates - apricots - almonds). It is one of the beautiful villages in the Afrin region (منطقة عفرين). The village residents also work in addition to agriculture in sheep and goat raising. Among its most important families is the Ali Agha family (عائلة علي آغا). The number of its population according to the civil registry dated 31/12/2004 is (1934 n).

Among the holders of higher degrees in the village: Sabri Hassan bin Hussein (صبري حسن بن حسين) / Doctorate in Economics / Aleppo (حلب) - Manan Sulaiman (منان سليمان) / Doctorate in Chemistry / France (فرنسا)

Village mayor: Hussein Hassan (حسين حسن)

II. Summary from Ax û Walat Transcript of Metîna

The village of Metîna, located in the Sherawa district of Afrin, holds a history stretching back approximately 300 years, its name originating from the nomadic Kurdish Metîna tribe from Northern Kurdistan. According to oral tradition, the village was first settled by three families—Selmo, Korik, and Nebênê—in a lower, swampy plain before being moved to its current location. The community's history is defined by resilience and communal work (hawarî), exemplified by the construction of its vital cisterns (sahrînc) around 120 years ago to solve a severe water shortage that forced women to travel to the neighboring village of Ma'shaleh for water and grain processing. This past, though difficult, is remembered as a time of greater blessing (bereket). In modern times, Metîna has become a sacred place (cihê pîroz) central to the Rojava resistance. In 2011, the historic shrine of Şêx Ûsiv was transformed into the Martyr Refîq Cemetery, which now serves as the final resting place for over 1500 Kurdish and Arab martyrs, including at least seven from Metîna itself. This deep connection to both ancient and recent history is also preserved through the individual efforts of craftsmen like Îbrahîm Xelîl Mihemed, who has been creating traditional tools and objects in his cave workshop since the late 1970s to ensure the village's material culture is not forgotten.

II. Summary from Ax û Walat Transcript of Metîna 2

The village of Metîna, located in the Shera district of Afrin canton, holds a rich history shaped by migration, resourcefulness, and sacrifice. According to oral history, the village's name originates from the Metîna tribe, a Kurdish group from Northern Kurdistan who were nomads associated with the snowy Metîn Mountain. The first settler was a man named Metîn, and the village was subsequently populated by various families and tribes, including the Bêrêş, Sûrk, Jelem, Reşî (from Çiyayê Reşa), Ne'îmî (originally Arab, from Şêx Oran), and Caferî (from Sînka). Originally situated in a swampy plain, the community faced a lack of water, forcing them to travel to the nearby village of Meşalê. This changed approximately 120-130 years ago when a prominent figure, Hec Nebo, and his sons constructed a vital water system, digging trenches to channel rainwater into cisterns, and also built the village's four traditional olive presses. This heritage of craftsmanship continues through residents like Ibrahim Xelîl Mihemed, a self-taught artisan who, since 1989-90, has created traditional stone and wooden items in his cave workshop, sourcing stone from the villages of Îska and Kefercenê. In modern times, Metîna has become a sacred place, defined by the Martyr Refîq Cemetery. Established in 2011 on the site of the ancient Henên Shrine—itself linked to Hanun, the brother of the Prophet David—the cemetery is the final resting place for over 1,500 Kurdish and Arab martyrs, including at least seven sons from Metîna who were martyred in battles at locations such as Qestel Cindo and Bab between 1989 and 2016.

III. Summary from other Channels' Transcripts of Metîna

A. Summary from Halil Sino Transcript of Metîna

Metîna, a village in the Afrin region, was established approximately 225-250 years ago on land that was formerly just tents and ruins, and was named after the Matina plain in Kurdistan. The village was founded by the Malê Selmo and Malê Nebê families, led by the brothers Selmo and Umo, who purchased the land from the people of Sharran village in exchange for six to seven years of its harvest. The community later grew with the arrival of the Jolian (Coliyan) family, whose ancestor, a Pasha, fled a conflict with the Ottoman Empire from the Jolia region in the mountains of Kurdistan and purchased one-third of the village. The position of village Mokhtar (chief) became hereditary within this family, passed down from grandfather to father to the current speaker, Nuri Hassan. Life in the past was characterized by significant hardship, with poor water and scarce work forcing residents to labor in other villages for payment in grain. The traditional diet was simple and healthy, based on local agriculture like bulgur (savêr) and lentils (nîsk), with meat being a rarity. The village's rich cultural life included vibrant wedding celebrations that lasted seven days and nights with traditional music (Def û Zirne) and dancing (Govend), a stark contrast to modern one-night events. A significant lost tradition was the winter gathering where elders would pass on oral history and epics like Rustemê Zal to the children. This historical period was also defined by the use of traditional farming tools like the ox-plow (jûtkêr) and sickle (mêjgal), and household items like the hand mill (destar), all now replaced by modern technology. Educational opportunities were limited due to oppression, often allowing only one child per family to attend school, which was located in a neighboring village.
