I. Summary from TirejAfrin Site

According to the book 'جبل الكرد' 'Mountain of the Kurds (Afrin)' geographical study by Dr. Mohammed Abdo Ali:

Beradê, براد /1408N - 350H - 14km - 585m/:

  • Abdullah Hajjar says that Brad means in Syriac "mail carrier - or place of running," /Mar Simaan al-Amoudi Church, page 176/. However, the rocky location of the village is not suitable for running sports at all. As for the book "In the Home of Mar Maroun- p21" it mentioned in addition to the above, that its original name in Greek is "Onioparas" meaning: Brad Spring or Cold Spring or Brad City Spring, but no village inhabitant mentions the existence of a "water spring" at the village site. As for al-Khouri Barsoum, he says that its origin in Syriac is: Barad, cold, village, town, from coldness due to its elevation, and its meaning is not mail carrier or place of running as is said, /p64/.

About the mail name, if the naming is correct, it is basically a Kurdish-Persian word, derived from the docked-tail mule Dûv birî which was assigned to carry mail to distinguish it and its function, then the name was transformed to "Bareed" / Yousef Saidawi - Syrian Television/. And Brad in Kurdish means: tremor or shaking and also means strike, and coin minting /Kurdistan Dictionary/, and this last name corresponds to the village being formerly a flourishing and important urban center during the Roman era and early Byzantine era, and capital of all Simaan Mountain sub-districts, and perhaps coins were also minted there so it was named thus.

  • A large village located on the middle section of Leiloun Mountain. It contains remains of church walls and collapsed buildings and massive carved limestone and columns and crowns and scattered capitals around the village, in addition to burials and wells carved in rock, all dating to the Roman and Byzantine eras. The Maronites believe that it contains the grave of the founder of the Mar Maroun sect.

And according to the book: Afrin.... its river and green hills by writer Abdul Rahman Mohammed from Qatmah village:

براد: A village in Leiloun and Simaan mountains, belonging to the central villages sub-district and Afrin region, Aleppo governorate.

A large village located on the western slopes of Leiloun Mountain and Simaan Mountain above a rocky plateau and slope with a valley starting from the west towards the east. The village houses are distributed in three areas: the northern side, the western side and the southern side. Settlement of the region is ancient, evidenced by remains of church walls and palaces and towers and crowns and guest rooms and stones and burials and capitals and markets and cisterns carved in rock. It is bordered to the north by a rough rocky mountainous chain and Kimar village, and to the south by a mountainous chain and path and rough area in the middle of a group of rocks and Kafr Nabo village and Burj Haidar. To the east by a rough mountainous chain with two paths and Miyasah village and Birj al-Qas, and to the west by the western Simaan Mountain chain and Afrin Valley at the bottom and Birj Abdul Wa and Baai villages. The number of its houses is about 275 houses and the age of the new and current village is 700 years. Its old dwellings are made of domes and stones mixed with clay and wood, while its modern dwellings are made of stones and reinforced cement. It has electricity network and paved roads connected to Kimar village via Basutah to Afrin. It has a modern mosque from the western side and an elementary school. The residents drink from wells and cisterns from rainwater in winter season and currently from artesian wells drilled next to the houses within the orchards. The inhabitants work in grain and olive cultivation and raising sheep and cattle. As for old Brad village, it is an ancient city and its antiquities indicate it, where there are several churches and ancient Roman towers, and these antiquities are located southwest of the village and the most important of these Roman antiquities are:

1- The Tawus dating to the 16th century AD.

2- The Roman burial in the center of the village from the 2nd century AD.

3- The ground burial 6 meters.

4- Archaeological house dating to the 4th century AD.

5- The Basilica Church dating to 561 AD.

6- The burial of Saint Mar Maroun for 410 AD.

7- Julia's Church for 399-402 AD.

8- The small church from the 7th century AD.

9- Stone guest rooms. 10- Monks' monastery.

11- The hermit tower and hermit column located in the southwestern side of Brad village.

12- Monastery tower and church and all these antiquities are scattered southwest of the village.

13- Residential houses and markets near Saint Marmaroun burial.

Currently the Ministry of Tourism and the General Directorate of Antiquities and Museums pay great attention to this site because of its important and great archaeological value, and Brad village is considered one of the largest archaeological villages in northern Syria and Aleppo city in terms of the antiquities found in it.

Village headman: Ahmad Abdo Aboud
