I. Summary from TirejAfrin Site

According to the book 'جبل الكرد' 'Mountain of the Kurds' (عفرين) Afrin City Geographic Study by Dr. Mohammed Abdo Ali (محمد عبدو علي):

Cilbir (جلبل) /1547N - 670E - 18km - 515m/:

  • Linguistically, we can say that the name is composed of two parts: Cil (جل) meaning clothing or carpet, and Bir or Ber (بر) meaning carpet or similar, and perhaps the name indicated what relates to carpets or clothing. But the closest to reality is that it is composed of these two parts: Cih (جه) meaning place and location, and Bel (بل) the elevated and upright, and the village is located at the highest point in an agricultural area surrounding it. The two parts merged to form a new name which is "Cihbel" (جهبل) or Cibel (جبل) and was written "Jalbal" (جلبل), then the letters "b" and "l" were replaced with the letters "l" and "r", so it became "Cilbir" (جلبر). The Kurdish word Cobel (جوبل) is known in meaning and is used to indicate a small elevated place.
  • A medium village located on Liloun Mountain (جبل ليلون). It is considered a center for the Al-Ghabari family (آل غباري), containing their main guest house. Beautiful modern and new houses were built in it. Its location is archaeological, as evidenced by the massive building stones in the village center. It contains a house belonging to former People's Assembly member lawyer Esmat Ghabari (عصمت غباري).

According to the book: Afrin.... Its River and Green Hills by writer Abdul Rahman Mohammed (عبدالرحمن محمد) from Qatmeh village (قرية قطمه):

Jalbal (جلبل): A village in Liloun Mountain (جبل ليلون), belonging to the Center Villages District (ناحية قرى مركز) and Afrin region (منطقة عفرين), Aleppo Governorate (محافظة حلب).

It is a small village located in the Ghabari area (منطقة الغبارية) (Al-Robari) and Liloun Mountain (جبل ليلون) on an agricultural plateau surrounded by forest trees and olive trees from all directions. It is bordered to the north by an agricultural fertile plain, Kashtaar village (قرية كشتعار), and Anab (أناب); to the south by a wide agricultural fertile plain and Jital Ziyara village (قرية جتال زيارة); to the west by a wide plain and mountainous chain planted with forest trees, Kobaleh village (قرية كوباله), Zuriqat (الزريقات), and Baslahaya (باصلحايا); and to the east by a wide agricultural plain, Kashtaar village (قرية كشتعار), and nearby Tanb (تنب). It has about 50 houses and its age is about 300 years. Its old houses are of stone, clay, and wooden roofs, and modern ones of stone and concrete. It has an electricity network, drinking water, and a paved road passing through its center to several neighboring villages including Kefer Jana village (قرية كفر جنة). It has an elementary school, a small mosque, and a telephone center. It belongs to Baslahaya municipality (بلدية باصلحايا). The village residents work in olive and grain cultivation and livestock raising. It is mentioned that the Al-Robari family (عائلة آل الروباري) lives in this village, and among its most important personalities known historically in the region is (Qasim ibn Sheikho Agha (قاسم بن شيخو آغا), owner of the village guest house), and his grandson Qasim (قاسم) still manages the guest house - Aref ibn Othman Agha (عارف بن عثمان اغا) - Dhahni ibn Othman Agha (ذهني بن عثمان اغا) - Said Othman Agha (سعيد عثمان اغا), father of (Professor lawyer Esmat Al-Ghabari (الاستاذ المحامي عصمت الغباري) who became a People's Assembly member more than once and has well-known social imprints in the region).

Among the scholarly personalities: Dr. Abdul Majid Sheikho (الدكتور عبدالمجيد شيخو) / academic holder of PhD in journalism and Dr. Mustafa Ibrahim Ghabari (الدكتور مصطفى ابراهيم غباري) / PhD in electrical engineering from France (فرنسا) - lawyer Ahmed Jawdat Al-Ghabari (الحقوقي أحمد جودت الغباري) - doctor Jamal al-Din Abdo (الطبيب جمال الدين عبدو) / People's Assembly member

And many holders of university degrees in various specializations from the new generation.
