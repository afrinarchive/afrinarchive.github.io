Summaries

I. Summary from TirejAfrin Site of Kosa (English)

According to the book: Afrin... Her River and Green Hills Geographic Study:

Kosa, كوسانلي, كوسان /1088n - 47h - 22km - 600m/:

  • كوسا: means "beardless chin". Name of a Kurdish clan from Rashwan (رشوان) tribe, there are some of them in Shahrazur (شهرزور) in southern Kurdistan (/Lerkh p. 57/). The mention of "Al-Kosah or Al-Kosiyah" (الكوسه أو الكوسية) clan has been recorded by Ibn Battutah (ابن بطوطه). There is a clan named Kasan (كاسان) in Rawanduz (/Lerkh, p.55/).
  • This small village is located on the northern slope of Bi'îvê (بعيڤه) elevation. It is about 2 km away from the Turkish borders. There are several small springs west of the village.

According to the book: Afrin... Her River and Green Hills:

كوسانلي: A village in Mountain of the Kurds (جبل الكرد) belonging to Rajo District (راجو), Afrin (عفرين), Aleppo Governorate (حلب).

It is a small village located in the northern section of the aforementioned mountain on the northwestern slope of Ba'ifah (بعيفة) mountain from which streams descend toward the northwest and west toward Black River (النهر الأسود) valley. Its soil is loamy and it is 2 km away south of the current Turkish borders and 22 km northeast of Rajo (راجو) town. It is bordered to the north by a deep stream and at 500m distance village Bandarki (بندركي), to the south by a high and rough mountainous chain planted with oak, oak, almond and mastic trees and called Almond Mountains (Ba'ifah) (جبال اللوز (بعيفة)) or Martyrs Mountain (جبل الشهداء) relative to a battle that took place between Syrian mujahideen revolutionaries and French occupation soldiers during independence and village Al-Walikli (الواليكلي), to the west by a deep stream and rough mountainous chain and Arab Li (عرب لي) farm and the (Meydan Ikbas - Bulbul) (ميدان إكبس _ بلبل) road, and to the east by a high mountainous chain from the northern Mountain of the Kurds (جبل الكرد) chain and village Shankali (شنكلي). The number of houses is 35 houses and the village age according to village residents' narration is about 400 years. Its old houses are stone-clay with wooden roofs and the modern concrete ones expanded toward the west and north. It has electricity network and elementary school and telephone network. The village drinks from cisterns that collect rainwater and from springs existing west of it and currently water has been extended to it through a network from a state artesian well. The residents work in rainfed agriculture (olives, vines) on a narrow area /47 hectares/, in addition to raising sheep and goats. Its lands are generally mountainous and rough from all directions with a deep stream separating the village from village Bandarakli (بندراكلي). This village was formerly considered among the Meydan (الميدان) villages.

II. Summary from Ax û Walat Transcript of Kosa

The village of Kûsa, located in the Rajo district of Afrin, holds a history dating back 900 years when it was one of seven villages established in an area originally called Telê Xozê (Pig Hill). Its inhabitants are from the Şêxî tribe, an identity they share with relatives in other villages of the same name across the border in North Kurdistan. The region contains significant historical sites, including the ruins of Keleha Sorê (Red Castle), which local tradition identifies not as a castle but as a Byzantine military camp built as a defense against northern raiders. This ancient structure, with walls two to three meters thick, was later a site of conflict involving the al-Hasini Kurds and was eventually damaged by an earthquake and fire. The village maintains distinct cultural practices, such as the creation of a traditional medicinal ointment called "Qopan" from local plants like evris and dehle, a remedy used for skin ailments in both humans and animals since before the availability of modern medicine. Kûsa was also the home of the poet Arif Xelîl, whose family continues to preserve his collections of poetry on Kurdish history, nature, and religion, ensuring the continuation of the village's literary heritage.
