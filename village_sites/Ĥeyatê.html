I. Summary from TirejAfrin Site of Ĥeyatê (English)

Villages of Mabetli District (المعبطلي) / Mabeta

Gu.Ĝeyatê (الحياة) - Gu.Ĝeyatê

===

According to the book 'جبل الكرد' 'Mountain of the Kurds' (Afrin City) Geographic Study by Dr. Mohammed Abdo Ali:

Gundî Ĝeyatê (الحياة), Hayat (حياة) /520N - 8km - 640m/:

  • Hayat (حياة): A local feminine proper name.
  • A small village located on the southern slope of Hawar Mountain (جبل هاوار). Some of its families left it and established a small residential gathering beside the public road Afrin City - Rajo (عفرين - راجو). It is a modern village dating back to 1963, and its original inhabitants came from Sêwiya village (قرية سيوية) located on the mountain top at a distance of about 2km.

According to the book: 'عفرين.... نهرها وروابيها الخضراء' 'Afrin City.... Its River and Green Hills' by writer Abdulrahman Mohammed from Qitma village (قطمه):

Al-Hayat (الحياة): A village in Kurdish Mountain (جبل الكرد), belonging to Mabetli District (ناحية المعبطلي), Afrin City region (منطقة عفرين), Aleppo Governorate (محافظة حلب).

It is a small beautiful village located in the central part of the mentioned mountain, on the southern slope of Hawari limestone mountain (جبل هواري الكلسي) which is crossed by streams. Its lands have gentle slopes spreading over its clay soil with forests and pastures. It overlooks the agricultural lands from the south and east directions. It is 13km away from Mabetli town (بلدة المعبطلي) toward the northeast.

Its construction is modern, dating back to 1963. It is bordered to the north by mountain elevations planted with olive trees and Uksuzli village (قرية أوكسوزلي), to the south by the fertile Katkh plain (سهل خصب) and the Afrin City Rajo road (طريق عفرين راجو) and Qitranli village (قرية قطرانلي), to the west by mountain elevations planted with olive trees and Kêl Îbo village (قرية كل إيبو), and to the east by a slope and agricultural plain planted with olive trees and Sheikh Kilo village (قرية شيخ كيلو).

The number of houses is about 35, and its age is about 42 years. It is a village of modern establishment. Its houses are stone and concrete spreading in all directions. It has electricity network and primary school. The village drinks from an artesian well near Kêl Îbo village (قرية كل إيبو) belonging to the state.

Its inhabitants work in olive and legume cultivation rain-fed and irrigated from artesian wells with summer vegetables and fruit trees (walnuts, pomegranates, apricots, apples), in addition to raising sheep and goats. Among its most important families is the Amiko family (عائلة عميكو) from neighboring villages, originally from Uksuzli village (قرية أوكسوزلي).

It connects to Mabetli town (بلدة المعبطلي) by a paved road to the village center.

Village mayor: Sabri Minan Minan (صبري منان منان)

II. Summary from Ax û Walat Transcript of Ĥeyatê

The village of Heyatê, located in the Mabata district on the main road between the Rajo and Afrin regions, was founded around 1961-1962 during a period of land reform known as "islah." Its founding families, including those of Ûsê Me'mê and Menê Hemkûsê, relocated from the nearby arid village of Sêviyê to their own agricultural lands, which offered a better life with fertile soil and access to water from newly dug wells. For this reason, they named their new home "Heyatê," the Kurdish word for "life." The village contains historical underground rooms known as "kulon," which were traditionally used to hide young men during times of conflict and conscription. The community's history is also marked by its contribution to the Kurdish freedom movement, with seven martyrs from the village, including members of the Xelîlçenê and Cemo families. Among them was the notable artist and guerrilla commander Şehîd Şakir, who began his artistic career in 1988, founded two music groups, and was martyred in the Dersim region in 1994 after attending the Masûm Qorqmaz Academy with his brother, Şehîd Heyder. The village's economy is a mix of traditional crafts and modern industry; a local craftsman named Neşet has been making wooden ladders ("sêlîm") for the olive harvest for over 30 years, while a factory producing plastic battery casings relocated to the village from Aleppo three years prior to the interview, bringing new work to the area.

C. Summary from Afrin 366 Transcript of Ĥeyatê

The village of Hayatê, a self-sufficient community of approximately 45 houses, is described as being over 400 years old. Historically, the land was owned by a group known as the "Şêx" people. About 150 years ago, the ancestors of the current inhabitants, including the Nasir family, migrated from the village of Kaxirê and purchased the land from the Şêx, who then relocated to the nearby village of Sêwiya. This historical connection is marked by significant religious and cultural sites in both locations. Hayatê is home to the ancient shrine of Şêx Seîd, a revered "man of God," which fell into ruin and was later rebuilt through funds collected by the villagers. Near this shrine stands a sacred tree where people tie pieces of cloth to make wishes for health, children, and prosperity. In the village of Sêwiya, there is an even older shrine dedicated to Şêx Hesen, the brother of Şêx Seîd, which is particularly renowned for its power to heal skin diseases. The cemetery in Sêwiya is situated within a protected oak forest, where a strong local tradition forbids the cutting of trees. Another local shrine, that of Şêx Ebdo, is uniquely located inside a house where visitors light candles and place stones to make vows, a practice passed down from ancestors. While these traditions are ancient, the area also shows modern developments, such as a new mosque built in Hayatê in 2023 and the ongoing craft of local artisans like Rêzan Ebû Ebdo, who makes tombstones for communities throughout the Afrin region.
