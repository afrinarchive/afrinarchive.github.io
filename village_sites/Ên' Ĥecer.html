And according to the book: Afrin.... Its River and Green Hills by writer Abdul Rahman Mohammed from Qatmah village:

Ain Al-Hajar Eastern (عين الحجر الشرقي): A village in Kurd Mountain (جبل الكرد), belonging to Mabetli District (ناحية المعبطلي), Afrin City region (منطقة عفرين), Aleppo Governorate (محافظة حلب).

It is a small village located in the central part of the mentioned mountain above a limestone plateau, gradually descending north toward Zarafkah Valley and crossed by streams ending in Afrin River valley to the west of Afrin river 1km away. It is 7km from Mabetli town. Its fertile agricultural land extends in the north and south. It is bordered to the north by Zarafkah River valley and fertile plain planted with olive trees and Anbarli and Sheikhutka villages, and to the south by a slope and fertile plain and Afrin-Rajo road and Arjum factories and Juwayq village, and to the west by a slope planted with olive trees and Omo or Sino farm, and to the east by a medium slope and large Ain Hajar village. It has approximately 65 houses and is about 200 years old. Its dwellings are stone and clay with flat wooden roofs, while the modern cement ones expanded to the south, east and west. It has an electricity network and primary school. The farm drinks from a water network that gets its water from Kokan Al-Tahtaniya well network near the public road. It connects to the district center and neighboring villages by paved roads to the village center. Its inhabitants work in rainfed agriculture on an area of 250 hectares (olives, grains and legumes) and they cultivate irrigated crops from artesian wells water in the southern plain with summer vegetables and fruit trees (walnuts and pomegranates), in addition to raising sheep and goats on the plateau slopes suitable for grazing. Among its most important families are Alush Aybash family and Beit Al-Biro (the first to inhabit the village).

Village headman: Nami Othman

III. Summary from other Channels' Transcripts of Ên' Ĥecer

C. Summary from Afrin 366 Transcript of Ên' Ĥecer

The village of Ên Hecer, which is divided into two parts known as Ên Hecer a Mezin (Big Ayn al-Hajar) and Ên Hecer a Piçûk (Small Ayn al-Hajar), derives its name from a local spring. Originally called "Kanî Kevirê" (Stone Spring) in Kurdish, the name was later Arabized to Ayn al-Hajar by local Arab populations, referencing a spring where water flowed from a stone into a stone basin. The village was established by Ehmedê Elî Ajo, who migrated from the village of Şiyê and settled the area after the departure of its previous inhabitants, the Welde Arab tribe. The community's history reflects a significant social shift from a more communal past, where large families lived together, to a modern reality of dispersal and emigration, leaving some elderly members feeling isolated. This is compounded by contemporary challenges, such as a severe drought that has caused crop failure and a lack of grazing land, impacting the traditional agricultural way of life. Despite these hardships, the remaining villagers maintain strong ties with their diaspora, expressing a collective hope for the rebuilding of their homeland and the eventual return of their relatives to their ancestral lands.
