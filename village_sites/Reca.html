I. Summary from TirejAfrin Site of Reca (English)

According to the book 'جبل الكرد' 'Mountain of the Kurds' (Afrin City) Geographical Study by Dr. Mohammed Abdo Ali (الدكتور محمد عبدو علي):

Reca, رهجا, حمو راجو /660n – 15km - 690m/:

  • It derived its name from Rec (رج), and it is believed to be a feminine proper name. One of the village residents from the nineteenth century was named Ḩusênê Recê (حسينێ رجێ), and the letter ê in Kurdish is a feminine letter, meaning that this Hussein was attributed to his mother Rec who gave her name to the village.
  • A small village located on the southern slopes of Khastiya elevations (مرتفعات خاستيا).

According to the book 'عفرين .... نهرها وروابيها الخضراء' 'Afrin City.... Its Rivers and Green Hills' by Abdulrahman Mohammed from Qatmah village (عبدالرحمن محمد من قرية قطمه):

حمو راجو: A village in Mountain of the Kurds (جبل الكرد), belonging to Mabetli district (ناحية المعبطلي), Afrin City region (منطقة عفرين), Aleppo governorate (محافظة حلب).

It is a small village located in the middle part of the aforementioned mountain, on the summit of a limestone plateau, marine, eroded and surrounded by water streams, and forests spread on its slopes. It is 12km southwest of Mabetli town (بلدة المعبطلي) toward the southwest.

It is bounded to the north by a mountain elevation planted with olive trees and Qashla village (قرية قشلة), to the south by a steep slope planted with olive trees and a deep stream and mountain chain and Mastu Ashur village (قرية مستو عاشور), to the west by 50m Ali Jaro village (قرية علي جارو) nearby, and to the east by a slope and Sili Valley (وادي سيلي) planted with olive, walnut, and almond trees and Jumazanli village (قرية جومازانلي).

Its soil is hilly with pastures and groves spread above it. The number of its houses reaches 35 houses and its age is about 250 years. Its old houses are of stone and clay with flat wooden roofs, while the modern ones are concrete spread on the outskirts of the village north and south.

It has an electricity network, small mosque, and elementary school. The village drinks from cisterns that collect rainwater in winter, and currently from artesian wells dug next to houses. The residents work in agriculture (150 hectares) rain-fed, producing olives, grains, and legumes in addition to raising sheep and goats. It connects with the district center by a paved road passing through its center to several neighboring villages.

Among its most important families: Hamo Rajo family (عائلة حمو راجو) which named the village after them.

Village mayor: Ramzi Haqi (رمزي حقي)

II. Summary from Ax û Walat Transcript of Reca

The villages of Reca and Êlicara, located in the Mabeta district of Afrin on a mountain known as Çiyayê Xwedê Gazîra, are two distinct communities that have merged into one, physically separated only by their shared mosque. The history of the villages is rooted in their founding ancestors. Êlicara, originally named "Jare," derives its modern name from Elî yê Carê of the Biyan tribe, whose four sons—Hec Birîm, Oso, Ehmed, and Şêxê—established the village's primary families. Similarly, Reca was named after its first settler, Reco Mezin, whose four sons—Hem, Nes, Henûn, and His—founded its core lineages, later joined by families like the Şeara and Mala Bekirê Reşo from the nearby village of Gorda. The area holds deep historical significance, evidenced by an ancient well dating to the Roman era that once supplied water to seven surrounding villages, and a nearby plane tree said to be 1300 years old. During the French Mandate, local men like Apê Remzî were recruited into the French army, serving for years in a specific Kurdish unit known as the "Eskadron Kurd Lêjê Lîxa." In a later era, under the rule of Hafez al-Assad, the original Kurdish names of the villages were officially changed by the Syrian state. The community's traditional life, which once took place in a landscape of dense terebinth forests, was self-sufficient; villagers like Apê Xelîl crafted their own agricultural tools, plows, and wooden spoons from local qetlê wood, as specialized carpenters were not available.
