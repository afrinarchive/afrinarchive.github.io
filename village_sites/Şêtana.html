I. Summary from TirejAfrin Site of Şêtana (English)

According to the book 'جبل الكرد' 'Mountain of the Kurds' (عفرين) Geographic Study by Dr. Mohammed Abdo Ali:

Šêtana (رحمانية), Rahmanieh (رحمانية) /1012n - 116ha - 13km - 650m/:

  • Its old name was "Shetana" (شيطانا), which was a description of the behavior of some of its inhabitants in the past, and the name was recently changed to Rahmanieh (رحمانية).
  • This small village is located on a hill covered with olive trees. Most of its inhabitants have emigrated in search of a livelihood.

According to the book: Afrin... Its River and Green Hills by writer Abdul Rahman Mohammed (عبدالرحمن محمد) from Qatmah village (قرية قطمه):

Rahmanieh - Shetana (رحمانية - شيطانو): A village in Mountain of the Kurds (جبل الكرد), belonging to Mabetli District (ناحية المعبطلي), Afrin City region (منطقة عفرين), Aleppo Governorate (محافظة حلب).

It is a small village located in the central part of the mentioned mountain on top of Hamo mountain (جبل حمو) which slopes steeply toward the southwest in the direction of Al-Risha valley (وادي الريشة). It is 12km away from Mabetli town toward the northwest. It is bounded to the north by Sari Oshaghi village (قرية صاري أوشاغي) at a distance of 500m, and to the south by a slope and deep valley planted with olive trees and Khaziyanli Tahtani and Foqani villages (قرية خازيانلي تحتاني وفوقاني), and to the west by a mountain elevation and Hamlurik village (قرية حملوريك) at a distance of 1km, and to the east by a mountain elevation planted with olive trees and Arab Oshaghi village (قرية عرب أوشاغي) and Kurkan Foqani village (كوركان فوقاني). The number of its houses is about 35 houses and 350 people. Its old dwellings are stone-clay with flat wooden roofs and the modern ones are cement extending toward the west and east. It has an electricity network and water network connected to artesian wells dug at the bottom of the valley to the south. It is connected to the district center by a paved road that passes through its center to several neighboring villages. There is a school in the village shared with Sari Oshaghi village (قرية صاري أوشاغي). The residents work in rain-fed agriculture on an area of 250 hectares (grains, vines, almonds, walnuts), in addition to raising sheep and goats.

Mr. Rashid Miqdad (السيد رشيد مقداد) (Abu Mohammed - one of the sons of this village) says that the history of our village is as follows: The naming of our village originally came from the first inhabitants who lived and took the cave as their dwelling. In front of the cave was a tree called in Kurdish "Siftaq" (سفتاك), and this tree, as is known and recognized by people in general, that snails stick to its branches, and snails in Kurdish are called "Shitonuk" (شيتونوك). Therefore, the name of the village came as it is known... And what many people do not know is that with the passage of days, its name became Rahmanieh village (قرية الرحمانية) after a story that occurred in it and is known by many of the elderly, which is:

In the past, there were merchants who wandered in the villages and they were from the Armenian sect working in the trade of antiques and gold... A well-known merchant used to frequent the village, and one day he came to the house of Mr. Haj Yasin (حاج ياسين) from the villagers and stayed there in his house. In the morning of the next day, everyone in the house woke up except the merchant due to his death. When Haj Yasin (حاج ياسين) learned this, he went quickly to the Mabetli police station and told them what happened to him, that the Armenian merchant died in his house... A patrol from the police came to our village and examined the body. After that, the villagers said he had a bag with him, and when they opened the bag, they saw antiques and old gold coins. The police were surprised by what they saw of antiques and gold, and based on that, the police chief at that time said we will change the name of the village from "Shetanu" (شيتانو) to "Al-Rahmanieh" (الرحمانية) because of the honesty, truthfulness, and mercy that the people in the village carry.

Village mayor: Mohammed Omar Mohammed (محمد عمر محمد)
