I. Summary from TirejAfrin Site of Çolaqa (English)

According to the book 'جبل الكرد' 'Mountain of the Kurds' - A Geographical Study by Dr. Mohammed Abdo Ali (الدكتور محمد عبدو علي):

Çolaqa (چولقان), Jolqan (جولقان), Jalaq (جلق) /1537N - 480m/:

  • Çolaq (چولاق): means "the lame" in Kurdish. Its first inhabitants used to live in the caves located north of the village, then one of them separated and was called "Çolaq" (چولاق), and he lived by the well at the current location of the village, so the village was named after him.
  • A medium village whose residential neighborhoods spread on both sides of a deep valley called the Valley of the Hyena (وادي الضبع) Ĝeftar (Ĝeftar). It was a prosperous, beautiful, and important village in the early twentieth century, inhabited by the Sido Mimi (سيدو ميمي) Aghas, which was their main center, and some of their descendants still live there. In the center of the village is an old archaeological well that still provides drinking water for the village and overflows in rainy years.

According to the book: Afrin.... Its River and Green Hills by writer Abdul Rahman Mohammed (عبدالرحمن محمد) from Qatmeh village (قرية قطمه):

Jolqan (جولقان): A village in the Kurdish Mountain (جبل الكرد) belonging to Cindirês subdistrict (ناحية جندیرس), Afrin (منطقة عفرين), Aleppo Governorate (محافظة حلب).

It is a small village located in mountainous land at the confluence of several streams where the stream and watercourse of the Jolqan spring (نبع جولقان) flow. Its soil is grassy and limestone. It is 14km northeast of the town of Cindirês (جندیرس). To the north, there is a high mountainous chain planted with olive and vine trees and Iki Akhour village (قرية إيكي آخور). To the south, there is the Sili valley (وادي سيلي) planted with olive, walnut, and almond trees and the shrine of Sheikh Abdul Rahman (الشيخ عبد الرحمن) and Sheikh Abdul Rahman village (قرية الشيخ عبد الرحمن). To the west, there is a high mountainous chain planted with olive trees and Kharzan village (قرية خرزان) and Ashkan East (اشكان شرقي). To the east, there is a high mountainous chain planted with olive trees and Kawrkan village (قرية كاوركان) and Qara Bash village (قرية قره باش). A stream runs through its center coming from the highest north towards the south to the valley and Cindirês plain (سهل جندیرس). Water flows in it as there is an old Roman well in the center of the village used by the locals for drinking. It has about 65 houses and its age is about 400 years according to one of the village's elderly residents. Its old houses are stone with flat wooden roofs, and the modern ones are concrete that spread east and south. It has an electricity network and a water network connected to the Kawrkan well (بئر كاوركان). The village previously drank from a Roman well located in the village. It has an elementary school and an old mosque. It connects with the region and subdistrict by paved road passing through its center to the neighboring villages. It administratively belongs to the municipality of Kawrkan (كاوركان). The residents work in olive and grain cultivation rainfed and irrigated from artesian wells with fruit trees such as walnuts, apricots, and summer vegetables, in addition to raising sheep and goats. The construction and establishment of this village is attributed to one family that inhabited it, which is the family of Khalil Batal Sido Mimi (خليل بطال سيدو ميمي), the most famous in the Afrin (منطقة عفرين) and they are the original village owners, as evidenced by the existence of four old and luxurious villas for them: two of them are located on the eastern side of the village, while the other two are located in the center of the village and are considered among the most beautiful old buildings remaining until now in the Afrin (منطقة عفرين) and the village in general is beautiful in terms of location and surrounded by trees and three mountainous heights from all directions. There are two olive mills in it located on the eastern side of the village.

Among the holders of higher degrees in the village: Hassan Sheikho (حسن شيخو) / Doctorate in History / Russia (روسيا) - Mohammed Hussein (محمد حسين) / Doctorate in Veterinary Medicine / Russia (روسيا)

Village Headman: Mohammed Aref Othman (محمد عارف عثمان)

III. Summary from other Channels' Transcripts of Çolaqa

C. Summary from Afrin 366 Transcript of Çolaqa

The village of Çeqalo, located near the villages of Tilkê and Anbarê, is divided into two sections: Çeqalê Jorin (Upper Çeqalo) and Çeqalê Jêrin (Lower Çeqalo). It is the ancestral home of the Batal family, as well as the narrator's mother's family. The video was created at the request of Dilşot Adnan Batal, a member of the Batal family living in Europe, who wished to see his family's home and village. This family home has a long history; the original stone house was built by Dilşot's grandfather, and his mother has lived there for 50 years. The village's cemetery, Turbê Çeqalo, is situated between the upper and lower sections and is notable for the cypress trees planted along its road by the state, which the local council protected by imposing a fine against cutting them. The presence of an olive press, "معصرة المختار الحديثة," indicates the importance of olive cultivation in the area. The narrator also shares a personal connection to the village, mentioning that his brother is a martyr.
