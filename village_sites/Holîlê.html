I. Summary from TirejAfrin Site of Holîlê (English)

Villages of Rajo District (راجو) / Reco

Holîlê (هوليلو)

According to the book جبل الكرد|Mountain of the Kurds / Afrin City / Geographic Study:

Gundî Holîlê, Holîlê (هوليلو), Hlîl (هليل) /792N - 81ha - 5km - 720m/:

  • Holîl (هوليل): A title known by the first owner of the village, as there is still a large family in the village named "Holîlî" (هوليلي) Holîl. Holîlan is the name of a Kurdish tribe found in Kermanshah (كرمنشاه) /Lerkh, p.75/, connected to the Kurdish Lor-Fili tribes.
  • This small village is located on the western slope of "Bilal Mountain" (جبل بلال).

According to the book: عفرين.... نهرها ورواييها الخضراء|Afrin.... Her River and Green Hills by writer Abdulrahman Mohammed from Qatmeh village:

Holîlê (هوليلو): A village in Kurdish Mountain (جبل الكرد) belonging to Rajo District (ناحية راجو), Afrin Region (منطقة عفرين), Aleppo Governorate (محافظة حلب).

It is a small village located on a rocky plateau on the southern slope of a calcareous mountain planted with oak trees on its slopes, overlooking to the south agricultural lands served by streams flowing southward. It is 3 km from the town of Rajo (راجو) towards the northeast, with poor pastoral soil. It is bounded to the north by a mountain chain and the village of Hajmanli (حجمانلي), to the south by a slope, valley, olive and grape trees and the village of Kuranli (كورانلي) and Haj Khalil (حاج خليل), to the east by a harsh slope, deep stream, high mountain chain and the village of Jarkhtle (جرختلي), and to the west by a slope, deep valley, high mountain chain and the town of Rajo (راجو). The number of its houses is about 25 houses and its age is about 300 years. Its old dwellings are stone-clay with wooden roofs, while the modern ones are concrete spread to the north. It has a modern electricity network and the village drinks from cisterns dug in front of houses that collect rainwater in winter. It has a telephone and modern elementary school. The village is affiliated with the municipality of Haj Khalil (حاج خليل). The residents work in rainfed agriculture on an area of 81 hectares (olives, grains, legumes, grapes) in addition to raising sheep and goats. The village connects to the district by an unpaved dirt road. There is a shrine of Sheikh Mohammed Ali (الشيخ محمد علي) in the village.

Among the families present in the village: Al Khushman (آل خشمان) - Al Holîlê (آل هوليلو) - Al Kîla (آل كيله) - Village Mukhtar Mohammed Youssef bin Akash (محمد يوسف بن عكاش). The village is known for having revolutionaries against the French occupation, most notably the late Hussein Samou (حسين سمو).

Village Mukhtar: Mohammed Youssef (محمد يوسف)

III. Summary from other Channels' Transcripts of Holîlê

C. Summary from Afrin 366 Transcript of Holîlê

The village of Holîlê holds a rich history, with its current inhabitants from the Birûkî tribe tracing their origins back approximately 350 years to a migration from Diyarbakir, by way of Weranşar. Upon their arrival, they purchased the land from its original inhabitants, the Holî tribe, who were Yazidis and for whom the village is still named. The landscape is marked by significant historical and modern features; an old cave within the village was once used by the people as a hiding place, and a prominent water tower was constructed during the French Mandate period, drawing its water from the Kanîya Gewrê spring. A stone plaque on one building, inscribed with the name Muhammad Ali, dates its construction to 1962. The village's geography includes views of the snow-capped Çiyayên Gewr (The White Mountains), the nearby Çiyayê Bilêlê (Mount Bilele), and the neighboring villages of Kîwura and Çeqmaqo. The elders recall a difficult period during their fathers' time when conflicts with "enemies and raiders," identified as the Şêxû and Husên groups, forced them to flee for refuge to Turkey and a place called Xirabê Şera. Traditional life involved subsistence activities such as gathering firewood from areas known as Şûtî and Bîban, and baking bread on a traditional saj (sêl).
