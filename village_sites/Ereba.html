# Ereba (عرب اوشاغي)

Villages of Mabetli District (المعبطلي) / Mabeta

Ereba (عرب اوشاغي) - Ereba

===

According to the book 'جبل الكرد' 'Mountain of the Kurds' (Afrin City) Geographic Study by Dr. Mohammed Abdo Ali:

Ereba - 'Erebê Ĝemşelek (عرب حمشلك - عرب أوشاغي), Awlad Al-Arab (أولاد العرب) /3331N - 410H - 3km - 580m/:

  • The claimant "Ḩemşelek" (حَمشَلَك), owner of the nearby Ḩemşelek village (قرية حَمشَلَك), owned agricultural lands at the location of this village, and he had workers of Arab origins, so he housed them there in caves that were suitable for living, then some of them built dwellings and settled there. The village was named "Arab" (عرب) and the name of the work boss "Ḩemşelek" (حَمشَلَك), then the village flourished with other local inhabitants.
  • A large village, located on the top of an elevation overlooking vast plains of olive fields.

According to the book: 'عفرين.... نهرها وروابيها الخضراء' 'Afrin City.... Its River and Green Hills' by writer Abdulrahman Mohammed from Qitma village (قطمه):

Arab Oshaghi (عرب اوشاغي): A village in Kurdish Mountain (جبل الكرد), belonging to Mabetli District (ناحية المعبطلي), Afrin City region (منطقة عفرين), Aleppo Governorate (محافظة حلب).

It is a large village located in the central part of the mentioned mountain in a high mountain location surrounded by slopes from all directions. Its soil is soft limestone. The slopes descending from it northward are covered with oak trees and pastoral grasses.

It is bordered to the north by a slope and the fertile Al-Katkh plain (سهل كتخ الخصب) planted with olive trees and the railway line Aleppo - Meydan Ekbez (حلب - ميدان إكبس) and Kêl Îbo village (قرية كل إيبو) and Hamshalk (حمشلك) and Korkan Foqani (كوركان فوقاني), to the south by a slope and stream watercourse and mountain elevations planted with olive trees and Barimja village (قرية بريمجة) and Silo farm (مزرعة سلو), to the east by a slope and plain planted with olive trees and Qantara village (قرية قنطرة) nearby and Mabetli town (بلدة معبطلي), and to the west by a slope and stream watercourse and Khaziyan Tahtani (خازيان تحتاني) and Foqani (فوقاني).

The number of houses is about 300, and its age is about 400 years. Its old dwellings are stone and clay with flat wooden roofs, while the modern ones are concrete that began spreading in all directions. It has electricity network and telephone and primary and preparatory school and mosque, and several modern olive presses and agricultural cooperative society.

It drinks from Barimja village (قرية بريمجة) water through a modern water network in addition to cisterns that collect rainwater in winter. It connects to Mabetli town (بلدة معبطلي) in its southeast by a paved road 3km long, and a road passes through its center to several neighboring villages.

Its inhabitants work in rain-fed agriculture on an area of 410 hectares, including 10 hectares irrigated from artesian wells with summer vegetables and pomegranate and walnut trees, cultivating rain-fed olives, grains and legumes, in addition to raising sheep and goats.

Among the families existing in the village: Al Hamshalk (آل حمشلك) - Al Kulin (آل كولين) - Al Jamki (آل جمكي) - Al Tashi (آل طشي) - Al Haso (آل حسو) - Al Ba'bo (آل بعبو) - Al Madina (آل مدينة) - Al Milla'ali (آل ملاعلي) - Al Marjan (آل مرجان) - Al Jamo (آل جامو) - Al Khair (آل خير) - Al Khalil Fandaq (آل خليل فندق) - Al Jalha (آل جلحه) - Al Khana (آل خانه).

Village mayor: Mohammed Yusuf (محمد يوسف)
