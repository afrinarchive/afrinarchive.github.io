Summaries

I. Summary from TirejAfrin Site of Dîkê (English)

According to the book جبل الكرد|Mountain of the Kurds (Afrin) Geographic Study:

Gundî Dîkê (گندی دیکێ), Dîk Oba Sî (ديك أوبه سي), Al-Dîk (الديك) /923N - 24km - 710m/:

  • The village name is attributed to the Dîku (دیکو) family, who were the first inhabitants of the village. The official Ottoman name meaning "community of Dîk."
  • A small village located on the shoulder of a high mountainous rocky terrain that extends in the form of a rocky cliff stretching from east to west.

According to the book Afrin... Her River and Green Hills:

Dîk Oba Sî (ديك أوبه سي): A medium-sized village located at the beginning of the upper southeastern steep slope adjacent to the summit of Mount Kilsî (جبل كلسي), carved in the middle of the northern section of the mentioned mass. It is located 25km southwest of Bilbil (بلبل) town. It is surrounded by valleys and streams from all directions, making access to it difficult. It is bordered to the north by a steep slope and the plain of Eshona (عشونة) village and Kilanli (كيلانلي) village, to the south by a slope and stream valley and a high mountain chain of Mount Hawarî (جبل هواري) and Tag Oba Sî (طاغ أوبه سي) village, to the west by a slope and stream valley planted with olive and walnut trees and Jetal Qoyou (جتال قيو) village, and to the east by a steep slope and stream valley and Eshona (عشونة) village.

The village has approximately 100 houses and is about 300 years old. The old houses are stone and mud with flat wooden roofs, while the modern ones are cement but few in number. The village has an elementary school. The village drinks from cisterns that collect rainwater in winter or from artesian wells dug beside the houses. The residents work in cultivating olives, legumes, and vineyards on an area of 100 hectares, in addition to raising sheep and goats. Some of them also work in making wood charcoal. The road from the village to the main road passes through Eshona (عشونة) village - an unpaved mountain dirt road. From the western side to Rajo (راجو) road, it is paved to the center of the village.

Village Head: Hanan Sheikho Hamo (حنان شيخو حمو)
