I. Summary from TirejAfrin Site of Meydankê (English)

According to the book 'جبل الكرد' 'Mountain of the Kurds' / عفرين / Geographic Study:

Meydankê, ميدانكي (ميدانكي), Al-Maydan (الميدان) /4969n - 255h - 10km - 360m/:

  • Maydan (ميدان): meaning the square in the Kurdish language.
  • It was formed from two parts: upper Jorîn and lower Jêrîn. It is a large village that after the formation of Medanaki Lake (بحيرة ميدانكي) became a beautiful tourist site, and commercial establishments increased in it. It is one of the prosperous villages, and has blacksmith and carpentry workshops, pharmacies, restaurants, and a health center. There used to be Medanaki waterfalls (شلال ميدانكي) on the Afrin River (نهر عفرين) course, and beside it a water mill that was still working, in addition to a bridge from the Ottoman period, all of which were submerged by the lake waters.

According to the book 'عفرين .... نهرها ورواببيها الخضراء' 'Afrin.... Its River and Green Hills':

ميدانكي (ميدانكي): A village in Kurdish Mountain (جبل الكرد) belonging to Sheran district (ناحية شران), Afrin (منطقة عفرين), Aleppo governorate (محافظة حلب), (4725 n).

It is a large village located in the northern part of Kurdish Mountain (جبل الكرد) on the southeastern slope of a limestone elevation near a watercourse that descends east toward the Afrin River (نهر عفرين). It is connected by vegetation and is 10km away from Sheran (شران) in the northwestern direction. It is bordered to the north by fertile agricultural plains planted with olive trees and Naza Aw Shaghi village (قرية نازا أو شاغي). To the south are a valley and the Afrin River (نهر عفرين) course or Medanaki waterfalls (شلالات ميدانكي) and the ancient Roman bridge on the river. Currently they are submerged by the waters of the Seventeenth of April Dam lake (بحيرة سد السابع عشر من نيسان) and Koba Lak village (قرية كوبه لك). To the east are slopes and the Afrin River (نهر عفرين) valley and Ali Bazanli (علي بازانلي) and Qarqin Large (قارقين الكبير). To the west are slopes and valleys and the Seventeenth of April Dam (سد السابع عشر من نيسان) and the Bulbul Afrin Aleppo road (طريق بلبل عفرين حلب) and Kamruk village (قرية كمروك) and Shurbah aw Bah Si (شوربه أو به سي).

The number of its houses is about 300 houses and its age is about 500 years. Its old houses are stone-clay with flat wooden roofs, and the modern ones are cement following the paved road west of the village. It has an electrical network and drinking water from the artesian well water network existing and drilled in Naza Aw Shaghi village (قرية نازا أو شاغي) and belonging to the state, a telephone center, elementary and preparatory school, mosque, and the newest thing in it is a municipal building. There are also several tourist cafeterias near the river and there are several paved streets, especially the main street extending from the formerly Roman bridge on the river to Medanaki village (قرية ميدانكي) from the northern side. The body of the Seventeenth of April Dam (سد السابع عشر من نيسان) is also located nearby to the west. It has several service shops and currently the village has become the connection point between the district villages and Bulbul district (ناحية بلبل) due to its location in the center of these villages. It has modern olive presses and several block factories.

It connects to the district center and the region by a winding paved road. It is followed by Dodirli farm (مزرعة دوديرلي). The village residents work in olive, grain, and vegetable cultivation and sheep raising. As for Medanaki waterfalls (شلالات ميدانكي), they are located in the middle course of the Afrin River (نهر عفرين) at a gorge of limestone rocks, its slopes are steeply sloping. Currently they are submerged inside the dam waters. The formation of the waterfalls is due to the hardness of the limestone rocks in the river bed, which leads to different levels of river slope by 5 meters and extending 80m. The residents estimated the waterfall body at 60m length, then an artificial dam was added to raise the water level and expand the area of the lake formed behind it. From the lake, an uncovered channel 200m long was derived to operate a water mill that still works until now, in addition to using the lake for irrigating neighboring orchards. A stone bridge from the Roman period was also built over the river between the waterfalls and the mill, which is still used for vehicle crossing. There are also several swimming cafeterias on the eastern side of the gorge. The state has carried out slope afforestation which encouraged the village residents and towns to frequent it for excursions. All roads to the waterfalls are paved. The Seventeenth of April Dam (سد السابع عشر من نيسان) was inaugurated in 2004 by Engineer Mohammed Naji Atri (المهندس محمد ناجي عطري), Chairman of the Council of Ministers. Water from this dam is used to irrigate the lands of Afrin plain (سهل عفرين), Jindiris (جندريس) and Juma (جومة) up to Mala Khalil village (قرية ملا خليل) near the Turkish border (حدود تركيا) and to bring water for drinking to the cities of Afrin City (عفرين) and A'zaz (أعزاز) and the villages adjacent to the water line.

Medanaki Dam (سد ميدانكي) (Seventeenth of April (17 نيسان)): On the way back from visiting the Qorsh (قورش) archaeological site (Nebi Houri (النبي هوري)), or if you are coming from Aleppo (حلب) via Kafarjannah Medanaki road (طريق كفرجنة ميدانكي), you must see the lake that formed behind the April 17 Dam (سد 17 نيسان) extending 15km eastward to Wirkan village (قرية ويركان) with an average width of 1km, and storing behind it 190 million m3 of water, sufficient for reclaiming 30,000 hectares of agricultural land with electricity generation capacity of 18 megawatts... The waters did not submerge the two remaining Roman bridges, the only ones in Syria (سوريا) since the Roman period until this date.

The Medanaki bridge (جسر ميدانكي) and the beautiful waterfalls have disappeared forever, and the water mill that was operated by the Afrin River (نهر عفرين) waters was lost. It would be wonderful if the subject of investing the shores of the new dam lake were studied rationally, so environmental reserves are established that are planted and invested as resorts, restaurants, and others until tourism activity is activated in the region.

Medanaki waterfalls (شلالات ميدانكي) / formerly: Waterfalls in the course of the Afrin River (نهر عفرين), belonging to Sheran district (ناحية شران), Afrin (منطقة عفرين), Aleppo governorate (محافظة حلب). Located in the middle course of the Afrin River (نهر عفرين) at 10m elevation southeast of Medanaki village (قرية ميدانكي), 1km away at a gorge of limestone rocks, its slopes steeply sloping. The formation of the waterfalls is due to the difference in hardness of limestone rocks in the river bed, which led to different levels of river slope by 5m and extending 80m. The residents extended the waterfall body by 60m length, then an artificial dam 150m long, 1.5m thick and 1m high was added to raise the water level and expand the area of the lake formed behind it. From the lake, an uncovered channel 200m long was derived to operate a water mill that still works until now, in addition to using the lake waters to irrigate neighboring orchards. A stone bridge from the Roman period was built over the river between the waterfalls and the mill, still used for vehicle crossing. There are also several swimming cafeterias on the eastern side of the gorge. The state has carried out slope afforestation which encouraged the residents of neighboring villages to frequent them for excursions, especially from Aleppo city (مدينة حلب) and Afrin City (عفرين) and neighboring villages. Currently the waterfalls, Roman bridge, and mill are submerged by the waters of the Seventeenth of April Dam (سد السابع عشر من نيسان) and nothing remains of these monuments. Several resorts have been established around the lake, the road to the waterfalls is paved, and currently there are no Medanaki waterfalls (شلالات ميدانكي) due to their submersion in the lake waters.

II. Summary from Ax û Walat Transcript of Meydankê

The village of Meydankê, located in the Sherawa district of Afrin, holds a history stretching back 300 years to its founding by a brother and sister, Sheikh Salih and Meryem. According to local elders, Sheikh Salih, also known as Abdul Qadir al-Jilani, was an ascetic who migrated from Baghdad, Iraq, first settling in the village of Sheikh Isa before his family established Meydankê. The village's social structure is built upon seven founding families—the Ûsika, Xocelera, Şêx Elî, Sabka, Çepera, Îmra, and Rehmana—all descended from Sheikh Salih's three sons. This deep-rooted heritage is actively preserved by its residents, such as Mihemed, a 70-year-old craftsman who has been creating sculptures from local materials since his childhood in the 1950s to keep old traditions alive, and Bavê Şivan, a drummer who continues a 25-year-old family craft as a service to his community, notably playing for hours at the funeral of a YPG martyr. In the modern era, the landscape and life of the village were significantly altered by the construction of the Meydankê Dam, a major project started in 1990 and completed in 2004, which created a 14-kilometer-long lake intended for irrigation and transformed the area into a well-known tourist destination.

C. Summary from Afrin 366 Transcript of Meydankê

Gundê Meduno, also known as Meydano Semolo, is an ancient village in the Rajo district of Afrin, with local oral traditions suggesting it is over 500 years old. Situated in a high-altitude region, the village's economy is significantly tied to the cultivation of high-quality cherries, which are harvested using a specific technique of keeping the stems attached to prevent spoilage and are sold directly to traders for prices ranging from two to four dollars per kilogram. The landscape is marked by significant historical and natural landmarks, including a large oak tree, locally called 'Kafnor', estimated to be approximately 400 years old, and the Ziyaret of Sheikh Mus, a prominent religious site and cemetery that serves the surrounding area, including the nearby villages of Gawundo and Dude. In recent times, the village has been affected by modern events, with houses suffering structural damage from the February 2023 Turkey earthquake. Furthermore, burial practices at the Ziyaret of Sheikh Mus have evolved, with specialized grave builders from Afrin and Aleppo now providing services to construct tombs, a practice that has been ongoing for at least three decades.

C. Summary from Afrin 366 Transcript of Meydankê 2

The village of Meydankê, described as being large like a city, has historically served as a central hub with its main road connecting to the surrounding villages of Bînê and Kîbor. It is a famous and well-known destination for tourism, though the speaker notes a significant demographic shift has occurred, with the current majority population now being Arab. The area is defined by the Meydankê Dam, which has undergone noticeable changes over time. The speaker recalls that in the past ("berê"), the water level was much higher, reaching the bottom of the bridge's concrete pillars, whereas now it has dropped significantly. A key piece of its industrial history is the now-defunct mechanism for operating the dam gates, which involved a cart running along a railway track on the bridge. Life around the dam was also different; a specific memory shared was of a restaurant that once existed on an island in the lake, which locals like the speaker and his friend Samir would visit by boat, a recreational tradition that has since been lost. The surrounding landscape features natural, layered rock formations, and the journey also identified the nearby villages of Gamrukê, Şêxûtka, and Emara.
