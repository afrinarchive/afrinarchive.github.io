I. Summary from TirejAfrin Site

# Elendara (علمدار)

According to the book 'جبل الكرد' 'Mountain of the Kurds' (Afrin City) Geographic Study by Dr. Mohammed Abdo Ali (الدكتور محمد عبدو علي):

Elendara (علمدار), علمدار (علمدار) /810n - 433h - 10km - 750m/:

The origin of the name Elê Darê (Elê Darê), and Ali (علي): he was the first resident of the location, who had pitched his tent beside a large cypress tree, so he was called "Ali, owner of the tree or the tree dweller" (علي صاحب الشجرة أو الشجري), as he also established a place for sitting on the branches of that large tree.

It is a small village located on the northeastern slope of "Bilal Mountain" (جبل بلال). It is the village of the mujahid Rasoul Rashid (رسول رشيد) who was executed by the French Mandate forces.

According to the book: 'عفرين .... نهرها ورواييها الخضراء' 'Afrin City.... Its Rivers and Green Hills' by writer Abdulrahman Mohammed (عبدالرحمن محمد) from Qatma Village (قرية قطمه):

Alamdar (علمدار): A village in Kurdistan Mountain (جبل الكرد) belonging to Rajo District (ناحية راجو), Afrin City Region (منطقة عفرين), Aleppo Governorate (محافظة حلب).

It is a small village located in the northern part of the mentioned mountain, on the northern slope of the Kilsi Heights (مرتفع كلسي) that slopes slightly northward, and overlooks to the north agricultural lands with alluvial soil. It is 7 km northeast of Rajo Town (بلدة راجو). It is bounded to the north by an agricultural plain and the (Bulbul-Rajo) road and Jilanli Village (قرية /جيلانلي/), to the south by a high mountain chain of oak and forest trees and Zarkanli Village (قرية /زركانلي/) and Jobanli Village (/جوبانلي/), to the west by a mountain chain and a rugged valley and Jaqmaq Saghir Village (قرية /جقمق صغير/), and to the east by slopes and streams and a rugged mountain chain and Kurzil Bulbul Village (قرية /كورزيل بلبل/). The number of its houses is about 35 houses and its age is about 250 houses. Its old dwellings are stone-clay with wooden roofs, and modern ones have spread on the sides of the village to the north and east. It has an electricity network and a modern elementary school. The village drinks from rainwater that collects in cisterns dug in front of the houses, and iron minerals abound in the rocks from the eastern side of the village, which is called in the region Rajo Iron (حديد راجو). It connects with the district center by dirt road to the public road. The residents work in rainfed agriculture on an area of 423 hectares (olives, vines, legumes, grains) and irrigated summer vegetables from artesian wells. This village was named after (Ali) who was the first to live in the village area among the trees, since the word (dar) in Kurdish means trees in Arabic, so the village's name became (Ali Dar) and then changed to Alamdar (/علمدار/). Thus Ali Dar (علي دار) is the founder of the current village. Among the village families are (Haj Rashid (حج رشيد) (Haji Alamdir (حجي علمدير)) - Mohammed (محمد) (Mohammadko (محمدكو))) and many university degree holders. It is mentioned that Professor Sheikh Mohammed Mohammed (الأستاذ شيخ محمد محمد) is among the first teachers in the Afrin City region (منطقة عفرين).

Village Headman: Mustafa Haji Rashid (مصطفى حجي رشيد)

II. Summary from Ax û Walat Transcript

The Elemdar tribe, originally from northern Kurdistan, began migrating around 1600 AD (03:40:44, 12:10:59). During the Ottoman Empire, the tribe was fragmented, with parts moving to various regions including the Hemqê plain (04:06:53 - 04:19:34). Their migration path led them from Hemqê to Kûrmêş mountain, then near Xerza village (where Şikefta Elemdêrê, Elemdar Cave, is located), followed by a settlement in Avrazê village, and later near a valley called Geliyê Çerqa (04:31:73 - 05:38:19, 12:19:64 - 12:47:22). The current village of Elemdarê was founded by Elî, a member of the Elemdar tribe; the village grew from the family of Hec Reşîd (06:05:56 - 06:32:84, 07:04:18). Historically, the village placed importance on education, with teachers sometimes brought from Kilês or children sent there to study in an old village school (08:23:95 - 08:47:53, 10:03:42 - 10:27:93). Notable historical figures include Reşîdê Resûl, who fought against the French and was executed (08:52:62 - 09:08:61), and Mihemedê Hecî, who was killed in Kilês around 1875-1880 for his beliefs, after which his wife, Mereme Cafe, managed family affairs (10:37:66 - 11:33:63). Before the Syrian crisis, about half the village's population lived in Helebê (Aleppo) (08:15:44). During the Rojava revolution, Şêx Ebdulrezzak Hec Reşîd lost his life for the Kurdish cause (24:21:23 - 24:26:46). The village also has several martyrs from this period, including Avdar, Silef Yegemîn, Dr. Şêrzad, and Emîn; the village commune is named after Şehîd Avdar (24:29:58 - 24:44:38). In the past, the Kurmêj mountain area consisted of olive trees and vineyards (14:48:73), and dimis (grape molasses) was widely consumed (14:56:22). The village's grape press (mehser) was formerly located to the east, in the old part of the village (16:07:85).
