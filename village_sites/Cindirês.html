Foundation/Origin Information of Cindirês

Located on the Roman road connecting Cyrus with Antioch. It developed from a small village north of Cindirês hill into a large town.

Source: TirejAfrin Site

Possible Village Name Meaning of Cindirês

Multiple theories: 1) From "Jand" (Islamic military division) and "Eres" (a Roman leader). 2) From the monk "Jindaros". 3) From Greek "Gindaros" or Syriac "Jindriyus". 4) From Aramaic "Jindra" (pride/horses) or "Jindra" (rocky knot). 5) A Kurdish folk narrative suggests "Jan Dêris" (body/soul + death/destruction) from a story of forced labor.

Source: TirejAfrin Site

Summaries

I. Summary from TirejAfrin Site of Cindirês (English)

According to the book جبل الكرد|Mountain of the Kurds (Afrin) - A Geographical Study by Dr. Mohammed Abdo Ali (الدكتور محمد عبدو علي):

Cindirês (جندیرس) /10599N - 231m/:

  • Opinions vary about the meaning of the name Cindirês (جندیرس) and we will mention here the most important ones:

The Syrian Geographical Dictionary says that it is a compound name of two words: "Jand" (جند), which is an Islamic military division, and "Eres" (إيرس), which is the name of a Roman leader, so the name becomes "Jand Eres" (جند إيرس).

There is another opinion that says it is the name of the monk "Jindaros" (جندراوس) Cindaros (Cindaros), and he built "in the first quarter of the fourth century AD, a temple in Cindirês (جندیرس) in honor of the monk Jindaros (جندراوس), which is the oldest temple in Antioch (انطاكية).

The researcher Abdullah Halo (عبدالله حلو) /p.201/ says: that the name Cindirês (جندیرس) appears in Greek sources as Gindaros (Gindaros), and in Syriac as Jindriyus (جِندَريوس), and here lies the difficulty in knowing whether the name was originally pronounced with an open first letter, and in this case it will go back to an older Aramaic form Jindra (جِندْرا) meaning: pride, horses, and superiority, which is correct as a metaphorical meaning for a village name. However, the name in Greek sources as Gindaros (Gindaros) reflects for us a pronunciation of the name with a broken first letter as in the Arabic pronunciation, and it is more correct that the Arabic pronunciation of the name is a tradition of the Greek form, so it may be either an inaccurate pronunciation of the Syriac form, or perhaps an inaccurate pronunciation of another Aramaic derivation with a closed first letter, namely Jindra (جُندْرا) which means: rocky knot, and this is also a possible possibility.

However, as is known, the location of Cindirês (جندیرس) is plain, with loamy soil, and there are no rocks in it originally. We do not believe that its location in the middle of the plain indicates a rocky knot or superiority, and therefore we believe that the Aramaic form is excluded.

However, we believe that these researchers went far when they did not hear the folk narrative about the origin of the name Cindirês (جندیرس), which says: that the name is Kurdish, and it has a narrative that states that one of its rulers in the past forced the people to carry soil to the hill located near the town, and he determined for each family an amount of it, so the hard work continued for a long time, exhausting the people, and causing destruction in the conditions of the village people, until it was called the village of "Jan Dêris" (جان دیرس) Candêris (Candêris), which is a Kurdish compound word from: Can (جان) meaning body or soul, and Dêris (دیرس) meaning death and destruction. Thus the naming is Kurdish with a historical narrative, and it is still said in Cindirês (جندیرس) that such and such family is indebted with so many loads of soil.

  • The location of the town of Cindirês (جندیرس) has been inhabited since ancient times, and it is located on the Roman road that used to connect the ancient city of Cyrus (سيروس) with Antioch (انطاكية). Cindirês (جندیرس) is surrounded by fertile plains, with numerous springs, and the Afrin river (نهر عفرين) passes 5km south of it, making its location one of the ancient settlement centers in the southern part of the Joumeh plain (سهل جومه).

The town of Cindirês (جندیرس) developed from a small village that was north of Cindirês hill (تل جندیرس) to a large, prosperous town, and its houses spread on perpendicular streets in the middle of a flat, spacious plateau wavy with olive trees. Cindirês (جندیرس) is connected to the city of Afrin City (عفرين) by a paved road 20km long.

Cindirês (جندیرس) enjoys great economic activity compared to other towns in the Afrin (منطقة عفرين), and there is a large migration to it from neighboring villages. Most of its residents work in agriculture, especially olives. It has many and varied commercial shops and olive mills, in addition to carpentry and blacksmithing crafts and workshops for maintaining agricultural machinery, vehicles, and construction supplies. It has a weekly market held every Monday.

According to the book Afrin... Her River and Green Hills by writer Abdul Rahman Mohammed (عبدالرحمن محمد) from Qatmeh village (قرية قطمه):

Cindirês (جندیرس): Cindirês (جندیرس) in the Kurdish Mountain (جبل الكرد) and the Cindirês plain (سهل جندیرس), belonging to Afrin (منطقة عفرين), Aleppo Governorate (محافظة حلب).

It is a large and ancient town inhabited by people on the old Roman roads that used to pass through it to Nabi Houri (نبي هوري) and Antioch (انطاكية). The town is located in the middle of fertile plains at an elevation of 231m, surrounded by numerous springs, and the Afrin river (نهر عفرين) passes 5km to the south. To the north, there is a fertile agricultural plain planted with olive and orchard trees and Yalanqoz village (قرية يلانقوز) and Kefar Safra (كفرصفرة) and Koran (كوران). To the south, there is a fertile plain and the Afrin river valley (وادي نهر عفرين), and Madaya village (قرية /مداياي/) and Al-Mahmudiya (المحمدية) and Tell Silur (تل سلور). To the west, there is a fertile agricultural plain planted with olive trees and the Al-Hamam road (طريق الحمام) and Iskandaroun bay (لواء اسكندرون), and Agjalleh village (قرية أغجلة) and Haj Iskender (حاج اسكندر). To the east, there is a fertile plain and the nearby Rifatiya farm (مزرعة رفعتية) and Hamilk village (قرية حميلك) and Hajiler (حاجيلر). It has about 1400 houses and its age is about 500 years. It was previously a small village around the archaeological Cindirês hill (تل جندیرس) located on the southern side of the town.

Cindirês (جندیرس) took its name according to a narrative from one of the village residents from a Roman commander named (Jineh Eros (جنه إيروس)) and it was a small village near an archaeological hill. Current Cindirês (جندیرس) is a large town whose houses spread harmoniously on perpendicular streets on a wide, flat area in the middle of a spacious plain wavy with olive trees. Its buildings have reached Yilan Qour village (قرية /يلا نقور/) in the north and its population according to civil registry records at the end of 2004 (2004) is about 10,200 people, however, its population is much larger due to the heavy migration from the subdistrict villages.

Its old houses are stone and clay with wooden roofs and the modern ones are made of concrete and stone. Among its most important streets: its main street extending east to west and passing through the center of the town, which is Martyr Bassel Al-Assad Street (شارع الشهيد باسل الأسد). It has an electricity and water network. It gets its water from an artesian well dug in the nearby Kefar Safra village (قرية /كفر صفرة/).

It has several elementary, preparatory, and secondary schools, a health center, a municipal building, a police station, a subdistrict directorate, an agricultural association, a party branch, a telephone center, and an agricultural extension unit. There are several modern olive mills in it, especially the olive mill located on the western side of the town. There are several commercial and service shops for the town from various professions such as the Halal market (سوق الهال) for selling agricultural products. The village connects with all its villages and the region center by paved roads connecting to all neighboring villages and the subdistrict and region center.

The population of Cindirês subdistrict (ناحية جندیرس) is 60,622 people. Among its most important families are the family of Nouri Arbo (نوري عربو) and Nouri Barakat (نوري بركات) and Al Tubal (آل طوبال) and Al Qeitrash (آل قيترش) (Haj Qader (حاج قادر)) and Al Khourshid (آل خورشيد) and Al Kareem (آل كريّم) and Al Houto (آل حوطو) and Al Qara Hassan (آل قره حسن) and Al Murad Ahmed (آل مراد أحمد).

Cindirês (جندیرس) is the town of the subdistrict center located in the middle of its villages. To the west, it is bordered by the Turkish borders and to the south by Idlib Governorate (محافظة إدلب) and the Mount Simaan region (منطقة جبل سمعان). From the north and west, there is Al-Maabatli subdistrict (ناحية المعبطلي) and Sheikh Al-Hadid (شيخ الحديد), and from the east, the town of Afrin City (عفرين).

33 villages and 29 farms belong to Cindirês (جندیرس) with an area of 32,510 hectares. It has the subdistrict directorate, a police station, city council, commercial market, commercial shops of all professions, a telephone center, cultural center, and car garage. Cindirês (جندیرس) enjoys notable economic activity compared to other subdistricts. It is an agricultural town whose residents work in agriculture, especially olive cultivation and some economic crops such as wheat, sugar beet, sunflower seeds, and others on the banks of the Afrin river (نهر عفرين). A weekly market is held in it every Monday. The population of Cindirês subdistrict (ناحية جندیرس) is 60,622 people.
