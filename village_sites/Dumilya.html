I. Summary from TirejAfrin Site

# Dumilya (دومبللي)

According to the book 'جبل الكرد' 'Mountain of the Kurds' (Afrin City) Geographic Study by Dr. Mohammed Abdo Ali (الدكتور محمد عبدو علي):

Dumilya (Dumilya), دومبللي (دومبللي), الأمسية (الأمسية) /2148n - 232h - 12km - 600m/:

Damliya (دملياa): A corruption of the name Donbllali (دونبلالي), which is the name of a Kurdish tribe found in Botan (بوتان) /Lêrx (ليرخ), p.48/, which lived under Safavid rule (الحكم الصفوي), then migrated to the Ottoman section (القسم العثماني) of Kurdistan (كردستان), and settled west of Diyarbakir (ديار بكر) and established in the city Palo (Palo) and its surroundings a semi-independent emirate /Kurdish Dictionary (قاموس كوراني)/. Danbal (دنبل) in Kurdish means hedgehog. According to 'كتاب المسالك' 'Book of Routes', the Danbali people (الدنبلية) were a people who inhabited the Inverted Mountains (جبال المقلوب) and Al-Mukhtar (المختار), and the persecution drove them to leave for the areas of Mosul (الموصل) and Azerbaijan (أذربيجان), and they were able, thanks to their cunning, to establish an independent administration in Kurdistan (كردستان) and Azerbaijan (أذربيجان). The Sharafnama (شرفنامة) says about them that they are from the Ezidi sect (النحلة الإيزيدية). Amin Zaki (أمين زكي) J1 p.403/, says this tribe is from Parazi (البرازية). Wasfi Zakaria (وصفي زكريا) says about them that they are from the Milli tribe (القبيلة المللية) /p.664/. The Arabized name is given by the Arabization Committee (لجنة التعريب).

It is a medium-sized village located at the foothills of the southeastern slope of Benê Mountain (جبل Benê), and is 2 km west of Badanli Village (ق.بعدنلي).

According to the book: 'عفرين .... نهرها ورواييها الخضراء' 'Afrin City.... Its Rivers and Green Hills' by writer Abdulrahman Mohammed (عبدالرحمن محمد) from Qatma Village (قرية قطمه):

Dombllali (دومبللي): A village in Kurdistan Mountain (جبل الكرد) belonging to Rajo District (ناحية راجو), Afrin City Region (منطقة عفرين), Aleppo Governorate (محافظة حلب).

It is a large village located on the southern slope (mountainous elevation and slope) of the central part of the mentioned mountain. It is bounded to the north by a high and rugged mountain chain and Darwish Oba Si Village (قرية درويش أوبه سي), to the south by a slope and valley and Kurkan Tahtani Village (قرية /كوركان تحتاني/) and Kurkan Foqani Village (/كوركان فوقاني/), to the west by a high and rugged mountain chain and Quda Koy Village (قرية /قوده كوي/), and to the east 500 m away Badanli Village (قرية /بعدنلي/). The number of its houses is about 200 houses and its age is about 400 years (400 عاماً). Its old dwellings are stone-clay with flat wooden roofs and the modern concrete ones have spread west, south and east. It has an electricity network and water network from an artesian well belonging to the state, and the village used to drink from a Roman well in the past. It has an elementary school and a mosque. The village connects with the district and region by paved roads. The residents work in rainfed agriculture (olives, vines, grains, and other fruit trees) in addition to raising sheep and goats. The village is considered one of the beautiful villages in terms of construction and location.

Village Headman: Bakr Ali Bakr (بكر علي بكر)

II. Summary from Ax û Walat Transcript

The village of Dimilya was founded approximately 500 years ago by three Zaza-speaking men named Mem, Os, and Mele (00:52, 01:35). Mem and Os were brothers who, along with their relative Mele, migrated from the Mehsertê region of Mêrdîn (01:46). They initially settled near Ehrez before moving to the current village site, where they lived in three caves and worked as shepherds (09:38). Over time, other families, such as those of Mela Zîno Kê, Mela Çekîş, and Mela Quro, joined them (11:14). The village, which started with three families, has grown to about 360 houses (13:11). Historically, the village faced conflict with aghas (feudal lords) from the neighboring villages of Bedîno and Memelo who tried to subjugate them, leading to killings (12:15). The village's first school was only built about 20-30 years ago; before that, children attended school in Bedîno (14:21). An elder mentions taking his final exams in Afrîn in 1967 (14:47). For centuries, the village relied on a large, rain-fed pond for all their water needs, which was crucial for the village's growth (21:58). Over the last 40-50 years, the villagers have dug individual wells, and the pond has fallen into disuse (22:25). An old stone olive press, or "delîp", was brought to the village by two men, including the father of Hesen, approximately 160-170 years ago from a hill called Tilê Cir Cirim (28:28, 28:45). It was used for pressing olives for 40-50 years until modern presses arrived, after which it was used for making bulgur wheat (29:44, 30:45). Many villagers used to live and work in Heleb (Aleppo), but after the start of the Rojava revolution, they began returning to the village to live and start businesses (34:27).

Summary from secondary Ax û Welat Video:

The village was founded approximately 500 years ago by three Zaza individuals (Mem, Mela, Ûs) who migrated from the Mêrdîn region (00:50, 01:38, 07:00). They initially lived in caves (10:16) and created a vital central pond ("gol") for water (10:41, 21:57), used for centuries for drinking, washing, livestock, and swimming (23:21). Seven other families later joined the founders (11:01). The village historically resisted external domination (11:56). A traditional stone olive press ("delîp"), potentially 500-600 years old, served as a community hub for processing olives and grain (27:06, 28:08), later replaced by modern methods (30:02). Rituals for rain were performed at a specific rock ("kevirê hêvîyê") involving sacrifice and communal meals (16:06, 17:11). Historically, many residents worked in Aleppo (Heleb) but returned following the Rojava revolution (34:27, 43:27). The village school was built relatively recently; previously, children attended school in Be'dinê (14:14, 14:29). Traditional knowledge includes assessing olive tree age by the pit (39:05, 40:05). The central pond became disused in the last 40-50 years due to well digging (22:22).
