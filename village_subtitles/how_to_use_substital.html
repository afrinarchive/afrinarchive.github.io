How to Use Subtitles with YouTube

A simple guide for the Substital browser extension

What is Substital?

Substital is a free browser extension that lets you add subtitles to any YouTube video. You can:

  • Upload any subtitles file (.SRT format) to any YouTube video
  • Adjust timing if subtitles are out of sync
  • Change subtitle size and color

Instructions for Chrome

1

Go to the Chrome Web Store:

chrome.google.com/webstore/detail/substital
2

Click the blue "Add to Chrome" button

3

Click "Add extension" when the pop-up appears

4

Pin the extension (recommended): Click the puzzle piece icon in the top-right corner, then click the pin icon next to Substital

How to Use Substital

Once installed, follow these steps for any YouTube video

1

Go to a YouTube video you want to watch

2

Click the Substital icon in your browser toolbar (top-right corner)

3

Click the video link that appears in the pop-up

4

Drag and drop your .SRT subtitle file into the pop-up window (or click the upload button to browse for it)

If Subtitles Are Out of Sync

Sometimes subtitles appear too early or too late. Here's how to fix it:

1

Look for the Substital button that appears at the bottom of the YouTube video player (after adding subtitles)

2

Click it to open the settings

3

Use the sync slider to adjust timing:

  • Slide left (negative) if subtitles appear too early
  • Slide right (positive) if subtitles appear too late

Customizing Subtitles

You can change how subtitles look from the same Substital button at the bottom of the video player:

  • Font size (make text bigger or smaller)
  • Text color
  • Background color
